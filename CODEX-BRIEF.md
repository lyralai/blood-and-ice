# CODEX BRIEF — Blood and Ice: Book One Revision

## What This Is

A fantasy novel manuscript (Book 1 of a trilogy) in first-person present tense from the POV of "Rook," a 12-year-old boy who escapes a murdered house, washes up on a Sothoryos-style death-island, is raised by monster-tribes, and ends in a fighting pit. The voice is feral, pre-verbal — Rook reads the world through bodies, not abstraction. Think Tarzan meets Red Rising meets First Law.

## Key Design Decisions (DO NOT VIOLATE)

### The Voice
- First-person present tense, feral, pre-verbal
- NO abstract nouns (hope, despair, justice) — everything through material analogy
- "I read it off him" = epistemology. "The body knows it before the head" = cognition.
- Rook had words on the beach (ch00) but LOSES them on the island. By ch01 he's nearly wordless. The tribe gives language back one sound at a time.
- Voice tics to watch: "body reads" (target: under 20), "leans/leaning" (under 25), "holding-center" (under 15), "the big one," "thick-armed," "the count"

### The Red / The Root
- NOT a passenger, NOT a demon, NOT a separate entity. The Red IS Rook.
- The island burned his survival-instinct down to bedrock. The "root" is the pure survival-core. The "after-things" (name, count, Kala's teaching, the still place) are built on top — newer, shallower.
- When the Red comes up: the root runs without the head attached. Memory gaps. The still place erodes.
- The pit feels like HOME because the root recognizes a pure survival-place. The horror is that Rook's own deepest wiring feels at home in hell.
- Cost: physical (cold, wounds reopen) AND psychological (memory gaps, still place weakening, root getting closer to the surface every time)
- Model: Abercrombie's Bloody-Nine, NOT Sanderson hard-magic

### The Sister
- She LIVES. She split from Rook before the Mure attack, rowing north to draw off pursuit.
- Rook doesn't know if she's alive, dead, or captured. This NOT-KNOWING is the engine.
- The sister is the proto-Kala. When the Kala finds Rook, it fills the sister's shape — not a grave, but a waiting.
- The sister can return in Book 2 or 3. Do NOT kill her offscreen.

### Monster Tribes (NOT human)
- **Vorth** — great-ape brutes. The family. High stones. Keep their young, keep their dead, keep fire. The Kala, Marrow, the seer, Voss-Marr, Gull-Marr.
- **Mure** — deep-one tide-dwellers. Pale, web-fingered. The horror-mirror: what the Red makes of a person if it goes too far. THE MURE SANK THE BOAT — they're drawn to warm blood. They couldn't eat Rook (his blood burned them).
- **Skell** — jackal-coded scavengers. The discarded. Grist is the first of Rook's Pack.
- **The Hesh** — apex predator. Not a people. Pure appetite.

### The Made-Hill (Blood Temple)
- Ziggurat with Eye-and-Thorn carvings and blood-channels. Built to channel/use the Red.
- MADE THE MURE — humans who went through the hill's blood-process and came out as deep ones.
- The island is a trap for warm-blooded things, designed around the Red.
- Currently a one-scene mystery in ch11 — needs to be more present.

### The Patron
- Recognized the Eye-and-Thorn on Rook's neck. Hands trembled.
- The patron's faction has a connection to the made-hill / the blood-trade.
- The patron wants the weapon (the Red), not the boy.

### The Kala
- STAYS ALIVE through Book 1 (original draft had her die; revision keeps her alive)
- Pirates chain her as leverage to control Rook
- She is Rook's Eo (forging wound) but doesn't die — the separation is the wound

Ch01 needs to be revised to flow from the end of ch00 (alone, the companions dead, turning into the green) into the survival section. The voice transition is: ch00 has words → ch01 loses them.

### 2. HIGH: Voice Continuity Pass
The revised chapters (ch00, ch11, ch18-27) and the originals (ch01-10, ch13-17) were written in different sessions. The voice needs to be consistent across the seam. Specifically:
- Ch00 has more language (Rook is 12, recently had companions, still has words)
- Ch01-ch10 have less language (Rook is going feral, words burning away)
- By ch07+ (when the Vorth find him) he's nearly wordless
- The tribe gives language back one sound at a time
- By ch11 he has some words back (naming Marrow, the seer scene)
- By ch18+ he has more (but still feral — reads bodies, not abstraction)

### 3. HIGH: Tic Reduction on Originals
Ch01-10 and ch13-17 have NOT had the tic reduction pass. Run these counts and reduce:
- "body reads" — currently ~28 in revised chapters, unknown in originals (likely high)
- "leans/leaning" — reduce throughout
- "holding-center" — reduce
- "the big one" — vary (there are multiple "big ones" — the pirate Hark, the Vorth, etc.)
- "thick-armed" — vary
- "the count" — reduce

### 4. MEDIUM: The Mure Thread
Plant the Mure more deliberately:
- **Ch00:** Done (they sink the boat, can't eat Rook)
- **Ch01-10:** Add a Mure sighting. Rook sees something pale rise from the channel and the body remembers the wrong-cold. The gut-drop dread.
- **Ch14-15 (the Hesh hunt):** The deep channel the Vorth won't cross = Mure territory. The Vorth taboo on the low water is race-memory.
- **Ch19 (slaver/pirate camp):** The caged Mure hits different now — it's personal. The shape that sank his boat.
- **Ch27 (the pit):** The pit's blood-stone connects to the made-hill. The channels. The feeding. The Mure are the product.

### 5. MEDIUM: The Made-Hill Expansion
Currently one scene in ch11. Expand:
- Rook goes further in before turning back
- Sees something in the basin (residue, stain, the mark of long feeding)
- The disc burns near the hill
- The seer's knowledge of the hill connects to the Mure (race-memory of what the hill did to blood)

### 6. MEDIUM: The Sister Thread
Rook thinks about the sister at key emotional moments:
- When the Kala first holds him (the body remembers the sister's hand)
- When the pirates take the Kala (the shape of loss repeating)
- In the pit (looking up, the way he looked north for the sister)
- Do NOT overdo it — 3-4 touches across the book, not every chapter

### 7. LOWER: Prose Polish on Static Chapters
Ch23 (caged) and ch24 (crossing) are the slowest chapters. Ensure Rook always has something new to READ — not just internal monologue. The voice strains in stillness.

## What NOT to Do

- Do NOT add human tribes. The tribes are monsters. This is locked.
- Do NOT make the Red a separate entity/passenger/demon. It IS Rook.
- Do NOT kill the Kala in Book 1. She stays alive.
- Do NOT kill the sister. She's alive offscreen.
- Do NOT reveal Rook's full heritage in Book 1. Mystery only.
- Do NOT add standard narration or switch POV. Single first-person feral.
- Do NOT soften the voice with abstract nouns or modern psychology.
- Do NOT explain the Red. Three theories stand, none confirmed.
- Do NOT make the pit the start of a fighting arc. It's the ENDING.
