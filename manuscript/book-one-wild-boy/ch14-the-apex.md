# Chapter 14: The Apex

There is a low when the green itself goes still, and the stillness is a thing with teeth.

I read it in the soles before the eye. Same as I read the deep rot's mouths, same as I read a tide turning in the channel. The high stones wake into a wrongness, and the wrongness is a quiet. The biting cloud that blankets every warm thing on Saqarro, the swarm that rings off my skin alone by a hand's width — even that thins and lifts and goes off into the high green. The Skell that dispute the kills at the rot-thicket's edge, gone from their middens all at once, like the small fish going out of a pool before the thing that eats the pool comes in.

The green has emptied itself ahead of a thing. I do not have its name yet. I have only the shape the emptiness makes, the hole in the noise where a hole should not be, and the body stands all the way up under it, the hair lifting on the arms, the make-yourself-nothing the hands beat into me waking again.

Something is coming up into my green that the green is afraid of.

---

The Vorth know it before I do, and how they know it is the worst telling of all.

I have read them the season through — loud, glad, grieving, the whole weather of them learned in the body where I learn everything. I have not known the tribe afraid. They are the strong, the great gray bulk and the main-strength close and the hand that turns a thing off a rock with no thought in it. Fear is the weak thing's weather. The thing I brought up among them. Not theirs.

This low they have it. It moves down the shelf from mouth to mouth, flat and pressed-down and small in the great chests — the sound a strong thing makes when it has met the thing that is stronger. The big ones gather the small ones in. The half-grown ones that cuff and contest the order of the stone every waking go quiet and crowd to the high rock.

Even Voss-Marr has it.

That is the telling I cannot read past. Gull-Marr's heir, the prime blood, the one they raise to hold the stone after — the one who looks through me like I am a gap in the air the day does not bother to fill — he stands among the half-grown with the fear pressed down in his big chest like the rest, and for one breath he is not above me at all. We are two things in the same green that the green has gone quiet around. He feels the eye on him and the head comes around, and the contempt comes back into the set of him. But it came back. It had to be sent for. That is new.

And up on the high clean shelf the seer stands with the ash-pale head lifted into the empty green, the pale finger up — not pointing at me this low, but out, beyond the broken lip, into the place the noise has fled. The head turns slow along the ridge-line, following a thing the eye cannot find, and makes the long low sound the keeper makes over the squared dead.

The seer is grieving a thing that has not happened yet.

---

It comes the next low, and I see what the green was afraid of.

I am down off the high stones at the channel-edge with the old male with the torn ear, the two of us reading the firmer mat where the burrow-things run, his strength set in my track. Marrow is up the bank above us, small and crooked on the high root where I put him to watch the open water — the one high shoulder cocked, the curled hand tucked to his chest, the quick dark eyes going and going where the big slow eyes do not think to look. He is my far seeing now. The strong hunt with their reach; I hunt with his eyes and the old male's arm and my own reading laid over the both.

And the green goes wrong around us between two breaths, a thing taken out of the world all at once.

Marrow feels it first. I do not see the water — I see *him* see it. The crooked body on the root goes stiff, the high shoulder drops, the curled hand works once at the air, and he makes the small hard click low in his throat, the one I taught him that means *here, look here, now.* The old male feels it the same breath. The torn ear goes flat, the great head comes up, and the small dark eyes that have learned to follow my reading do not follow it this low; they fix off into the steaming dark under the drowned giants, and the great body that turned a Vorth's whole strength to my aiming goes stiff and small and gathered, a thing that wants only to be elsewhere and knows it cannot.

Then the rot-thicket parts, and the thing comes through, and I learn the shape of the apex of my green.

It is wrong-made, the way the worst things here are wrong-made, but wrong-made *large* — built long and low and heavy to the ground, longer than the old male and me laid end to end, the dark hide slick and ridged and beaded with the rot-water it has dragged itself through. It moves slow and certain and without any hurry at all, because it has never once in its life met the thing it had to hurry from. The head is the wrongness the eye fixes on and will not leave — broad and flat, the jaw running back the length of the skull, the teeth not the clean teeth of a thing that takes one bite and is done but the teeth of a thing that takes and holds and rolls and does not give back, row on row, set for the long killing. The small eyes ride high on the flat skull, set to watch the water and the bank both at once. They are not the flat eyes of the king that weigh a thing and let it stand. They do not weigh. They have never once had cause to.

It does not look at us as a thing to be reckoned. It looks at us the way the tide looks at the shore: a thing it will reach when it reaches it.

The old male makes the pressed-down fear-sound deep in the great chest, and folded into it is the one word — a low hard shape the tribe has thrown down the shelf since the green went quiet, the name the Vorth lay on the thing that runs them: *the Hesh.* And then the great strong body that I have aimed like a thrown stone turns and goes. Not at the Hesh. Away from it, up the bank, fast, the main-strength bent the one way it knows to bend when the eye cannot save it. He does not look to see if I come; the strong does not wait on the weak when the apex is in the green. I am left at the channel-edge with the thing coming on, slow, certain, and the hunger in me gone silent over the long low body, struck silent the same as it went silent over the dying fat-meat beast — not with wanting. With the knowing of meat that has met the thing that eats it.

---

I do not run. The body does not let me run, and it is not bravery; it is older than bravery and lower down. It is the hold-cunning, the make-yourself-nothing the hands made in me in the dark. The old male ran, and the running is a moving thing, and a moving thing wakes the high eyes set to watch the bank. So I do not run. I go still, the whole of me, low to the firm mat, a stone that does not move, the way I crept under the notice of the hands in the hold. And the high eyes pass over the place where the nothing is and follow the moving thing instead, the long heavy body shifting its weight after the old male, and the channel-edge where I lie goes empty of its attention, and I am spared.

I am spared the way the swarm spares me. The Hesh drags its long death past me a spear-throw off, the high eyes blind to me, and even pressed flat in the soup I am straining at the *why* — why the thing built for the long killing drags its weight past the one small soft thing in reach and does not turn the broad head. I want the knowing of it so hard my teeth ache. It will not come. There is no word in me for it and no breath to chase it with, only the flat fact of being alive and unfound where the strong is run to ground.

It does not catch the old male. He is too far up the firm bank, and the Hesh is a thing of the water and the soft edge and does not climb to the high stone. It stops where the firm ground begins, the high eyes fixed up the bank, and it makes a sound.

I have nothing to lay against the sound. It is low, lower than the king's lowest, felt in the soles before the ear, rolling up out of the great long body and over the drowned green into every living throat at once. The pressed-down fear that moved down the shelf — this is the thing that put it there. Far up on the high stones the whole tribe goes silent under it, the silence of meat that has heard the thing that eats it name the bank its own.

The Hesh has come up into my green to say the green is its green, and the high stones have heard it said.

---

I learn it that low, lying in the soup until the long body has dragged itself back into the rot-thicket: I am not the apex here.

I had begun to think it without the head ever saying it. I had stood up onto a ground that held my weight before the whole tribe, aimed the strong like a thrown stone, been named *this one*, laid by the Kala among the kept dead. Somewhere under all that keeping a thing had stood up in me that thinks I have bled a standing into the green that is mine.

The Hesh breaks it. The standing I earned is a standing *among the Vorth* — a thing the tribe gives, the eye against the strength. The green gave none of it. It has its own apex, and the apex does not weigh and does not let stand and does not ask. Beside it the whole main-strength of the tribe is meat that runs, and I am meat that lies still, spared only because it is too small to be worth the turning of a flat broad head. There is a height above the high stones I have only just learned to stand on, and the thing on it does not know I am alive. That not-knowing is the only standing I have against it.

---

I go up to the high stones in the dark, and the tribe is changed, and the change is the second telling of what has come into my green. They are crowded close, the way they crowd when the rains bring the wet rot into the small ones' chests, the gladness gone out of them. The bold small one finds me at the lip, but it does not ride my shoulder; it presses into me and goes still against my chest, and it is not the running-to-be-kept of the glad lows. It is the running of a small thing to a big one to be put between it and the dark — and I am the big one now, the keeping-place, and there is nothing I can put.

Marrow comes up the rock behind me, the rocking crooked gait quick even on the broken stone, and folds down at my flank where he has taken to folding. He does not crowd, the way the frightened tribe crowds. He sat the root and watched the long body name the bank and watched me lie unfound in the soup, and there is a thing in the set of him, in the quick dark eyes going from me to the dark and back, that is not the tribe's flat fear. It is closer to a question. He is waiting, the high shoulder low, to see what the small thing that was spared will do with the sparing.

The Kala folds down on my other side, the great gray-silver bulk, and lays the keeping arm over me, but the still-sound she makes is not whole. *Be still, you are held* — and the *nothing comes* dies in the great chest half-made, because something has come, and she will not make the sound over me that the green has already made false. She makes instead the low pressed fear-sound, soft, into the warm, and I understand it the body-way: she is afraid. The thing that chose me out of the fever-fog when I was a wrong thing the green could not finish, the warm wall at my back the whole season — she is afraid, for the tribe and for me, the smallest, the softest, the one the green has the easiest claim on of all of them.

And up on his broken lip the king does not stand turned to the bank, marking the place the weak thing learned to stand. He stands turned out, into the dark where the Hesh has gone, the heavy flat-eyed bulk of him still, and I read the thing off him like weather, bone-sure before the sky shows it. Gull-Marr is afraid too. The king whose law is strength, whose stone is strength's own marrow, has met the one thing his law has no answer for, and there is no brand laid in the flat tongue that will turn the long certain weight off the ground it has claimed.

I lie in the warm and read the no-answer off him, and a thing turns over in me, low down, where I read everything.

---

I do not sleep that low. I lie with the small one against my chest and the Kala's fear soft in the dark and turn the thing over, the way the body turns a thing it must solve.

The strong run from it. A thing that meets the Hesh strength to strength is rolled and held and does not come back, and a king who is rolled is no longer king. So the king will do the strength-answer: hold the high stone where the long body does not climb, let the green take from the edges of the tribe what it takes, and call it the law — the strong always call the thing they cannot help the law.

And I am not strong. It is the first thing I knew among them and the truest. But I have learned the one law the king's law has no room for: there is a way that is not strength, and the weak thing has it.

The Hesh is greater than all the strength there is. It is too strong to be met. It is not too strong to be read. It is too strong to be fought. It is not too strong to be brought — the way I brought the old male up out of the deep rot, not by the arm but by reading the ground it stood on and making the green itself the thing that took it.

I do not look at the thing straight, because the body has decided it before the head will say it, and the body's deciding sits lower than the place where looking is. The strong will hold the high stone and feed the Hesh the edges. I will not. There is a thing in me that the green has not broken, that the hold did not break, that the hands did not find behind the gap where the three teeth are gone, and it will not lie still and let the thing that named the bank its own take from the edges of the only home I have ever had.

---

The seer comes to me before the light, and I strain at it the low it happens, the same as I strained at the finger on my throat — reaching for the meaning and shut out of it. The ash-pale head folds down at the lip where I lie, the small old eyes going over my face the long reading way, and then the pale finger comes up and touches the eye bound at my chest — the ringed looking, the thorn driven through it, the Eye-and-Thorn off the dead expedition's relic the seer reads as a thing that comes from before and means. The seer makes the long meaning-sound, the one for the kept. Then the finger lifts and turns and points out, into the dark where the long body has gone, and makes the sound again, the *means*, laid on the place the green emptied itself ahead of.

I push at it and the head comes a long way after and does not arrive. The seer is laying the two sounds side by side — the mark at my chest and the thing in the dark — and folding them into one telling: that the wrong thing the green could not finish is the answer the strength-king has not got. The seer is not afraid the way the tribe is afraid. *This one, and that one, and the two are one telling.* I want the whole of it. I get the shape and not the inside, and the not-getting is a stone in the throat.

The far mouth stands up under it all the same, deeper than the bank's *this one* fed. To be read by the one that reads the old signs — read as the answer to the thing the strength cannot answer — fills me and frightens me both at once, because I know the shape of what the seer has laid on me, and it is the shape of the Hesh, and the body has already decided to go after it.

---

I lie in the warm until the light comes gray through the broken green, and I keep the deciding behind my teeth with the rest, in the gap where the name was, beside the brand I will give back someday across the water.

The Kala sleeps at last, the great slow tide of her under my ear. The tribe will wake and crowd closer and hold the high ground and feed the Hesh the edges and call it the law. And I will not. The home is real because the thing that eats it is real, and the two came up the green together.

But the bank over the drowned thicket taught me the harder thing too: the standing I earned and the cost of it came up out of one root, and there is no setting one down without the other. The green has sent up the thing that takes from the kept. I am the one thing in the whole frightened tribe that will not hold the high stone and let it take.

And the small scarred thing that reads the ground and throws the vine lays down the new law, the hardest the high stones have taught it yet, behind the teeth where the name lies:

*The thing too strong to be fought is not too strong to be read. And a home is a thing you do not hold by being strong enough to keep it. It is a thing you go up into the dark for, knowing you are not.*

No standing on the high stones is ever the last one; each one only buys the ground you go further from. And the further has teeth now, set for the long killing, and high eyes that do not weigh, dragging its certain weight through the rot-thicket of my green and naming the bank its own. The king will not go after it. The strong will not go after it. Voss-Marr, born to hold the stone, will hold it — and feel the contempt come back when he has to send for it, and not know why the sending is harder than it was.

I will go. Not this low. Not the next. But I will read the ground it stands on, and find the line up the wall the strong cannot climb, and make the green itself the thing that takes it — or it will take me, and the Kala will carry the small scarred wrong thing up to the high clean shelf and square it and face it to the sun among the kept.

I lie in the warm with the small one's quick heart and the Kala's slow vast tide and Marrow folded at my flank, awake, watching the dark with the same quick eyes he turned on the water. Over the second mouth, full and frightened both, I lay the going-up. And out beyond the broken lip in the dark the Hesh drags its slow certain death through the place I have bled a home into, and does not know yet that the smallest thing in the green has marked it back.
