# Chapter 27 Draft: Road Teeth

The first road broke before the second storehouse opened.

That was Maren's word for it. Broke. As if roads were bones.

Salt Veyr came into camp with one cart, two limping carriers, and a boy whose face had been pressed into gravel hard enough to leave black stone in his cheek. Their trade flags were still tied high. That made it worse. Flags were promises people could see from far away.

Someone had seen and taken anyway.

Maren did not shout. She touched the wounded boy's chin, turned his face toward the light, and looked at me.

"Your road."

Not a question.

Harvek said, "Which road?"

"Lower pass, below gull rock. Blood-crown guard."

The camp grew quiet in the way a mouth goes quiet before biting.

Senn checked his board. "Dorrik's watch."

Dremm's knot moved again. Not much. Enough.

I saw Keff among them, the red-haired boy from the spit. He would not meet my eyes.

The Wound-Guest woke with clean pleasure.

Here, it pressed.

This was easier than cradles. Easier than spears. A hurt boy, a broken flag, a named offender. The old answer fit the hand.

"Bring Dorrik," I said.

No one moved fast.

That told me more than obedience would have.

Solek went himself and came back with three young Khar Vane. Dorrik was broad, pocked, maybe seventeen. He had a necklace of gull bones and a face trying to be stone. Beside him stood Keff and a girl called Runa who had laughed at Dremm's songs two nights before.

Maren's people stiffened.

The wounded carrier spat blood. "They took oil."

Dorrik said, "Road gift."

Maren's face did not change. "Road gifts are asked by beggars and kings. Which are you?"

Some Salt Veyr laughed.

Dorrik flushed.

Keff said, "We guard the pass. Guard eats."

"Guard eats from agreed store," Maren said.

Runa lifted her chin. "Salt always agrees itself rich."

That was not entirely false. Truth makes poison stronger.

Harvek watched me with old interest. Dremm watched like pain waiting to be given meaning.

I wanted to hit someone.

The Wound-Guest wanted to break Dorrik's jaw, then Keff's, then make Runa kneel in the road with the oil poured over her hair so everyone would remember.

It offered the image kindly.

I walked to the cart instead.

One oil skin remained. Two were missing. The axle had been cracked where someone jammed a spear haft through the spokes. Not robbery in hunger. A lesson. They had wanted Salt Veyr slowed, embarrassed, reminded that roads pass under teeth.

"Who ordered it?" I asked.

Dorrik said nothing.

"Who ordered it?"

His eyes flicked.

Not to Dremm.

To Harvek.

The old chief's face became unreadable.

A small cold opened behind my ribs.

Harvek did not need to order a toll. He only needed young guards to believe old Khar Vane law would protect them if they took one.

This was not disobedience. It was a question asked with bruises: Did the crown mean Salt terms, or did it mean Khar appetite with a prettier name?

I turned to Harvek.

"Did you sanction road gifts?"

The camp stopped being quiet.

It became empty.

Harvek's hands hung loose at his sides. "Careful."

"Answer."

"I told them guards do not starve while traders roll fat through Khar pass."

Maren whispered something that sounded like a curse and a prayer sharing the same teeth.

The Wound-Guest surged. Old male challenge. Public. Blood required.

Harvek had chosen the ground well. If I backed down, Salt Veyr knew my roads were words. If I struck him, Khar Vane might split before winter. If I punished only the young, everyone would understand whose lesson had been protected.

Asha was not there to call me back.

Cairn was not there with the wrapped spear.

Only my name in my own mouth.

Rook.

The heat slowed.

I faced the camp. "Road guard eats from crown store after cradle tenth and before war share. Not from carts. Not from flags. Not from faces in gravel."

Harvek smiled without warmth. "You make another store from air?"

"No. From fines."

Maren's eyes sharpened.

I pointed at Dorrik, Keff, Runa. "They rebuild the axle. They return double oil from their family shares or work it off carrying Salt loads until the tally clears."

Keff opened his mouth.

"And they do it unarmed."

That hurt them. Good.

Then I looked at Harvek.

"You pay too."

A sound moved through Khar Vane. Not quite outrage. Not quite delight. The island loves danger when someone else stands inside it.

Harvek said, "Do I?"

"You taught the shape. They made it bloody. One war-chief share to road store before your hearth eats."

His eyes went flat.

The Wound-Guest begged.

Please, it almost said.

That was the worst thing it had done yet. Not command. Not hunger. Plea.

It wanted Harvek.

So did part of me.

I said, "Or say the road is Khar Vane only and Salt Veyr stops carts by sunset."

Maren did not miss her cue. "We can."

Harvek looked at the cart, the wounded boy, the young guards, his own people, the storehouse smoke.

He had built his life on knowing when meat had too much bone to bite.

"One share," he said.

Dremm made a broken sound.

Harvek turned on him. "You want hungry roads? Eat stones."

That saved me from looking grateful.

The punishment took all day. Dorrik and Keff lifted the cart while Runa set the axle under Salt instruction. Salt Veyr made the work exact and humiliating. Khar Vane watched their own made useful without weapons.

Near dusk, the wounded boy with stone in his cheek walked to me.

"Road holds?"

His voice shook. He hated that it shook.

"Road holds," I said.

He looked past me at Harvek.

"Even under teeth?"

The Wound-Guest listened.

So did Harvek.

"Especially there," I said.
