# Chapter 16 Draft: Bone-Mouth's Lesson

Harvek woke me before dawn by kicking the shelter post.

The whole hide roof shivered. Smoke dust fell in my mouth. I came up with a knife I did not remember taking.

Harvek looked at the blade, then at me.

"Good," he said.

I kept the knife up. "Why?"

"Because a chief who sleeps like a fed dog wakes as meat."

He walked away.

That was how Khar Vane gave invitations.

I followed because refusing him inside his own camp would have made a story too large for the morning. Otta saw me go and cursed under her breath. Dremm sat beside a low fire with his back to us, sharpening an axe that did not need sharpening. He did not turn.

Good, I thought, and then was ashamed because part of me liked that I had hurt him.

Harvek led me above the camp to the black ridge where old wind had peeled the stone into shelves. From there I could see the island making itself out of fog: Salt Veyr smoke low by the water, Hollow Antler woods white and still, Fen marshes holding night longer than the hills. Khar Vane fires burned behind us, bold as insults.

Harvek squatted on the ridge edge and handed me a strip of dried meat.

I smelled it before taking it.

He saw and grunted. "Goat."

"You could lie."

"You could eat."

Both were true. I ate.

For a while he said nothing. Harvek had the rare strength of not filling silence to prove he owned it. Most leaders I had seen used words like dogs use piss: to mark everything.

At last he pointed down to the camp.

"What do they want?"

I looked. Warriors moved between shelters. Children fought with sticks until a one-armed woman separated them by the ears. Solek spoke with two old men near the law racks. Yara sat apart, combing bone beads through her hair. Dremm sharpened. Always sharpened.

"Food," I said.

"Always. What else?"

"War."

"Some."

"Me."

Harvek smiled without warmth. "No. They want what you let them be."

I did not understand.

He took the meat strip from my hand, broke it in two, gave half back. "Dremm wants you hungry because hungry makes him holy. Solek wants you bound because bound keeps law alive. Yara wants you terrible and beautiful because terrible and beautiful travels farther than true. Otta wants you breathing because she hates waste. Maren wants you useful. Asha wants you answerable. Vessa wants you named small enough to cage."

He pointed at his own chest. "I want you dangerous in the direction I choose."

That I understood.

"And me?"

"You want not to be eaten."

I laughed once. It hurt my rib.

Harvek did not laugh. "That is not enough."

The words struck harder than Cairn's hand.

"It kept me alive."

"Alive is a shore. Not a boat."

I hated him then because he was right in a shape I could not bite. Survival had been everything. On the beach, in the trees, under Salt rope, in the pit, alive was the only crown. But men were gathering around my breathing and calling it law, omen, road, war. If all I wanted was not to be eaten, the hungriest person near me would decide where we walked.

Harvek picked up a loose black stone and set it between us.

"This is you."

He picked up smaller stones and put them around it.

"Khar Vane. Salt. Fen. Antler."

Then he used one finger to push the center stone forward. The smaller stones shifted after it.

"Leader," he said.

He pushed the smaller stones first. The center stone rolled because they crowded it.

"Meat with legs."

I stared at the stones.

"Which am I?"

Harvek stood. "Today? Meat that has begun to suspect."

He kicked the center stone off the ridge.

I watched it fall, strike once, vanish into fog.

"People who follow you will ask permission to become their worst selves," he said. "They will call it loyalty. They will call it faith. If you say yes because yes feels like power, they own you."

Dremm's disappointed face came back to me. So did the ring. So did the Wound-Guest studying my yield.

"If I say no?"

"They test whether you mean it."

"If I fail?"

"Then you learn what kind of meat a failed god makes."

He began walking down the ridge.

I stayed a moment, looking at the places hidden by fog. Hollow Antler had made silence into a weapon. Fen-Kin had tested my body for its owner. Salt Veyr counted every mouth. Khar Vane waited below, wanting me to be answer.

I did not want to be answer.

I wanted the beach before people. Hunger without witness. Cold without meaning. A knife was honest when held by a man who wanted your food. A tribe was a knife that asked you to thank it for the handle.

The Wound-Guest stirred.

It liked Harvek.

That should have warned me more than anything else.

When I returned to camp, Dremm was waiting near my shelter.

His axe was gone. His hands were empty.

"You yielded," he said.

"Yes."

"Why?"

Because I was afraid. Because I chose. Because if I had not, Cairn's throat would have opened in my hands and everyone would have loved me for it.

"Because he did not need killing."

Dremm's face moved. Pain first. Then anger covering it like hide over bone.

"Enemies need killing."

"Cairn came as messenger."

"He came to shame."

"Then why give him what he wanted?"

That stopped him.

Not enough.

"Khar Vane does not kneel to Fen games," he said.

"I did not kneel for Fen."

"I saw knees."

"Then your eyes are stupid."

Several people heard. A few sucked breath. Dremm's hand twitched toward the axe that was not there.

I stepped close enough that he could hit me without moving his feet.

"I knelt to keep my teeth my own."

He searched my face for mockery and found none. That confused him more than insult.

"The thing below would have won," I said. "You would have cheered. Then it would know how to buy you."

Dremm swallowed.

Behind him, Harvek watched from beside the law racks.

Dremm leaned close. "Maybe I want what came back from the pit."

"Then follow it into the dark and leave me."

His face went pale under the scars.

I had meant to wound him. The words did. More than I wanted.

He turned and walked away.

Yara, sitting by the fire, murmured, "That line will travel."

I looked at her.

"Do not make song from it."

She smiled sadly. "Rook, everything you do now is already leaving you."

I thought of Harvek's stones.

Leader.

Meat with legs.

That evening I moved my sleeping place from Harvek's shelter to the space beside the law racks. Not inside them. Not under his roof. Near enough to be seen. Far enough to be answerable.

Solek watched me lay the hide down.

"That place is cold," he said.

"Good."

"That place is for people waiting judgment."

"Better."

He nodded once.

No one moved me.
