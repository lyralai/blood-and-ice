# Saqarro and the Peoples — World & Culture Canon (Book 1: "Wild Boy")

> **Canon status: AUTHORITATIVE for Book 1.** Single source of truth for the
> tropical death-island, its monster-peoples, the slavers, and the blood-mystery
> as they stand on the page in *Wild Boy*. Supersedes the frozen-world doc
> `docs/world/four-tribes.md` (retained only as a Book 2–3 quarry; see the
> closing section). Where this doc and any older note disagree, this doc wins.

---

## 1. The Locked Frame (read first)

- **Climate is fully TROPICAL.** Saqarro is a Sothoryos-style fever-jungle:
  heat, rot, venom, monstrous fauna. There is **no ice, no snow, no winter** as
  weather anywhere in Book 1. The word "cold" in this trilogy never means
  climate — it means the **absence of blood** (see §6, the Bloodless).
- **The magic unifies on BLOOD.** "**The Red**" is the dormant, then awakened,
  active form of a blood-power. In Book 1 it is **dormant** — one faint omen at
  the very bottom of the pit (ch28), never a power-up.
- **The Red stays UNSOLVED on the page.** Three contradictory in-world theories
  stand and none is confirmed (see §5). Write to the mystery; never explain it.
- **POV:** Rook, first-person present, no human speech (his words were taken; he
  reads the world off bodies and weather). The peoples have no human names for
  themselves — Rook learns sounds, then lays his own words on things.

## 2. Saqarro — the Tropical Death-Jungle

Saqarro is the island Rook washes up on: a vast green hell that kills with
**fever, rot, and venom**, ruled by monstrous fauna and three monster-peoples.
It is not contested ground between humans and monsters — it is a place humans
**do not survive**, which is the engine of the whole book. Every human who has
ever landed here has died of the green. Rook is the one exception, and that fact
is the first on-page clue to his heritage.

### Texture (the four killers)

- **Heat.** A wet, standing heat that "lies on everything like a wet hide." It
  does not relent at night. It is the medium the other three killers travel in.
- **Rot.** The jungle eats everything that lies down in it — the dead
  expedition, the fallen giant trees, the still pools gone "to soup." Shelter is
  always shelter *against* the rot, never with it. The rot breathes **fever-air**
  up out of dead water all night.
- **Fever (the shaking-sickness).** The signature human-killer. It rides in on
  bad water and the rot-breath ("the seep"), comes **late and all at once**, and
  lays everything down. It put no mark on the dead men in the Wreck. It is what
  every human dies of and Rook does not.
- **Venom & the fauna.** Clear-glass fish, biting things in the dark that turn a
  limb "black and hot to the hip," the carrion-swarm, the apex monster (the
  Hesh, §4d). The green is actively predatory; it "decides to kill" on its own
  schedule and the thing that fights it dies fighting.

### Why humans die and Rook does not (the load-bearing mystery-seed)

Rook's **blood is wrong**. It burns out infection, closes wounds too fast,
shrugs off venom, and walks him through fevers that kill every other human.
On the page this is shown, never named:

- The carrion-swarm boils over every living and dead thing in the jungle but
  **rings him at a clean hand's-width** and will not land.
- He has "burned before" — venom, fever — and each time woke **whole and fast,
  faster than a thing should mend, faster than the dead men mended, who did not
  mend at all.**
- The Wreck's bones (§5) quietly prove the rule: **everything human dies of the
  fevers he doesn't feel.** The slavers (§5/§6) will sicken and die of the same
  seep even as they carry him off — the dramatic-irony countdown.

Rook himself **strains toward** the why and is thwarted — there is no word in
him for it and no one to ask. (Do not write him refusing to wonder; write him
reaching and the answer not crossing.)

### Saqarro's geography (working landmarks, not gazetteer)

- **The high stones** — broken pale rock breaking through the canopy "like bone
  through a wound." Sun-scoured ridges and shelves above the drowning green;
  where the Vorth tribe lives, out of the rot's reach. A man can read the whole
  low country from them.
- **The low country / the green** — the drowning jungle proper: canopy, dark
  rivers of shade, rot-thickets, still bad water.
- **The brown channel & the warm shallows** — tidal swamp-channels where the
  Mure rise; the swamp pulls back on the tide and bares its gleaning-stones.
- **The Wreck** — a dead older expedition's camp rotting in the mangrove on the
  old high-water flat; source of Rook's relics and the Eye-and-Thorn sigil (§5).
- **The blood-temple** *(mid-book deepener, folded into ch11, not its own ch)* —
  an interior jungle ziggurat: Eye-and-Thorn carved everywhere, blood-channels
  cut in the stone, where the peoples once fed *something*. The dead expedition
  died reaching for it. It **deepens** the mystery; it never solves it.

## 3. The Monster-Peoples — Vorth, Mure, Skell

> **DESIGN PRIORITY (the eval flagged these three blurring): keep them DISTINCT.**
> One axis fixes it — **each people answers a different question about Rook, and
> occupies a different rung of the corruption ladder (§5).** Use the contrast
> table, then the per-people entries. Rule of thumb: **Vorth = the family he can
> have; Mure = the warning of what blood makes of a man; Skell = the leavings he
> values when the strong throw them away.**

### Contrast table (the distinctiveness spine)

| | **VORTH** | **MURE** | **SKELL** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Body** | Great-ape brutes; upright, knuckle-walk; heavy shoulder, long arm | Deep-ones; pale, wet, web-fingered; tide-dwellers | Carrion scavengers; lean, low, jackal-coded, ribby |
| **Element** | The high stones (above the rot) | The warm shallows / the deep (below the tide) | The scree & the kill-sites (the margins) |
| **Society** | A *people*: king, kept young, tended dead, a tribe | Tide-pack; alien, near-silent, drawn by warmth/blood | Loose, broken, driven; read the order to survive it |
| **Role to Rook** | The found family / belonging engine | The horror-mirror: what the Red makes of a man | The discarded he gathers (the Pack) |
| **Ladder rung (§5)** | (baseline peoples) | **Beast** — the Red gone too far, a man drowned | (the leavings; not a rung — the grist) |
| **Rook's read** | "Warm to warm. I want." | Dread; "the gut drops out of me" | Kinship-in-leavings; "leavings, as I am leavings" |
| **Keyword** | KEEPING | DROWNING | GRIST |

### a. The Vorth — great-ape brutes, the people who take Rook in

The heart of the book. The Vorth are a **people, not a mass** — "a people made
of ones, each one its own." They are the engine of belonging and the Tarzan
found-family.

- **Body & ways.** Great-ape brutes: upright, heavy through the shoulder, long
  in the arm, going three-legged on a knuckle when they choose and easy up again
  — "a thing that owns both ways of going." Dark matted hide. Far bigger and
  stronger than a man. They live in the **high stones**, above the rot, where
  men cannot follow and the fever cannot climb.
- **What makes them a people (the on-page proofs).** They have a **king** (the
  longest tooth; the heads tip toward him). They **keep their young** — small
  ones scooped up off the rock and folded against the warm, never set down to
  die. They **do not turn out the weak** — the dragging leg is given the slow
  place, where a herd would leave it. They **keep their dead** — the squared,
  faced rows laid out on the cleanest high shelf in the first sun, come back to
  and tended. And they keep **fire** — a banked red light, fed and tended, that
  does not go out. Each of these is a thing Rook does not have and has no word
  for: "Only the hole where the word would go."
- **Tongue.** Sound thrown across the green — calls with "edges, sounds set
  against sounds," and a vocabulary of *gentling/keeping* sounds, *means*-sounds
  (laid over the dead and over omens), kill-cries. Rook learns it off their
  tongues "one low at a time," and language/self return to him **through the
  tribe**, not through any human. He later lays his *own* human words back onto
  things (his first chosen word-claim is naming Marrow, §4).
- **Why they spare Rook (the recognition).** They **witnessed** him survive the
  fever-death that lays everything down — carried up a burning man-shape to die
  the way their own die, and it did not die. His **wrongness reads to them as
  omen/kin**, not as meat: "a thing the green could not eat." The heads tip; the
  line bends around him.
- **The "old-blood" people.** The Vorth are the people whose **Seer senses the
  old blood in him** (§4e) and who carry their own recognition of the
  **Eye-and-Thorn**. They are the thread that ties Rook's heritage to the island.

### b. The Mure — deep-one tide-dwellers, the horror-mirror

The Mure are **not set-dressing** — they are the book's hidden warning, and must
be **dramatized**, not just named (eval fix). They are reframed as the
**Beast rung** of the corruption ladder (§5): *the Red gone all the way — humans
the blood took, what Rook could become.*

- **Body & ways.** Deep-ones: pale, wet skin; **web-fingered** hands; they
  **rise** in the warm shallows and the brown channels and dwell in the deep
  under the tide. Near-silent; their breath "whistles wet and wrong through the
  web of the throat." Alien where the Vorth are legible — a man cannot read a
  Mure off its body the way he reads a Vorth.
- **What they are (held back, never confirmed on the page).** Author-truth: the
  Mure are **what people become when the blood-power takes them too far** — once
  warm, once human-shaped, drowned. On the page Rook only feels the **dread**:
  when slavers haul a slack gray-silver shape up the mud, "the gut drops out of
  me," because for one breath he reads it as Vorth before he reads the web-
  fingers and the wet pale skin. The reader assembles the warning; no one states
  it.
- **Role.** A standing warning hidden inside an existing monster: the far end of
  the road Rook's own blood could walk. Also part of the slavers' **blood-haul**
  (taken alive, caged) — which is its own clue about why this island's monsters
  are worth a slave-ship.

### c. The Skell — carrion scavengers, the leavings

The Skell are the **margins** people, and the third must-dramatize (eval fix).
Their keyword is **grist**: what the strong grind off the bone and leave behind.

- **Body & ways.** Lean, low, ribby carrion-scavengers; **jackal-coded** — they
  pour to the death, they read the order of the kill, they live on what the
  strong waste. Where the Vorth keep and the Mure drown, the Skell **scavenge
  and survive** by reading the order that nearly kills them ("the leavings always
  read the order first").
- **Role.** The discarded that gather to the king who values what the strong
  throw away. **Grist** — a spared Skell, his grind-marks still on him — is the
  first of Rook's **Pack** (§4f), and the Skell as a people are nucleus material
  for Book 2's "unite the bled." They are also part of the slavers' haul (a
  broken Skell in a cage, §5/§6).
- **Motif tie.** Skell ↔ **Marrow** ↔ **Grist**: the rich thing inside the bone,
  and the grindings off it — "both are the leavings." This is the weak-and-smart
  spine made flesh across three names.

## 4. The Vorth Tribe & Its Cast

The tribe is built to read like a crucible (Red Rising's Institute / Blood
Song's Order / GoT) — it forges Rook a **band and a nemesis** who become the
saga. Locked supporting cast:

### a. The Kala — the adoptive monster-mother (Rook's Eo)

The keeper. A great gray-silver Vorth past her strength, who sits **to the side
of the high stones with the laid-out dead** and **tends them** — the one who
keeps. **She lost one** (a small one, laid among the squared dead). Out of all
the green, she leans her grieving want toward the burning man-shape on the
channel-stones, scoops him up, and folds him in against the warm "that knows it
holds me." Her sounds — the **gentling/keeping sound**, the **still-sound** ("be
still, you are held, nothing comes") — are the first true belonging Rook has
ever had. "We lie down in each other's hole." (Vorth tribe-word for her role:
Rook calls her the **Kala**.)

- **She is Rook's Eo (the forging wound).** In Part Five the slavers **bait the
  trap with her** — she is his most legible attachment, so she becomes the exact
  vulnerability. Rook **reads the trap, springs it knowingly to save her** —
  caught because he cared, not fooled — and **she dies anyway.** That death
  raises the trilogy stakes: strip the anchor and the rage (the Red) gets louder.
- **Design note (devastation, not assertion):** the bond must be built in
  concrete non-repeating beats across the middle (a shared act with hands in it;
  one friction — her keep-him-safe vs. his climb toward the crown that gets her
  killed) so her death lands earned.

### b. Marrow — the Brother (the keystone of the ensemble)

A Vorth **runt**, nearly culled at birth; survives on **wit**, like Rook. His
tribe-noise name is a dismissal; **Rook renames him "Marrow"** in the human
tongue as speech returns (ch9) — Rook's **first deliberate word-claim** (the
still-sound "got out" without him choosing; *naming Marrow* is the first time he
chooses a word and lays it on a thing on purpose — practice for freeing his own
buried name). Marrow = the leavings inside the bone; rhymes with Grist.

- **Body language (his whole voice — read off the body, never thesis-dialogue):**
  one **high uneven shoulder**, a **curled hand** drawn to the chest that opens-
  and-closes when he means *no*, a **rocking gait**. The high shoulder dropping
  is his *no*.
- **Forge (ch10):** the tribe means to drive Marrow out to the Skell; Rook saves
  him with a **trick, not strength** — together they rig the noose/deadfall and
  take a beast the strong hunters couldn't. Small+smart beats big+strong. From
  here he is Rook's shadow and the first of the Pack.
- **The warning voice (Part Four):** while Rook's man-shaped hole pulls toward
  the slavers, Marrow's body says, all night, **"your own kind is not your kin"**
  — the monster more human-loyal than the humans. "The kin is the runt going up
  the rocks ahead of you."
- **The pit (ch28):** taken in the same net, thrown in the same pit **as
  fodder** (the slavers knew the value of only one and threw the runt in as meat
  for the prize). The closing "I am not the only thing in here counting back" is
  **Marrow** — the band already two; Book 2 opens not-alone. Keep it hard (Kala
  dead, crown gone, the Red flicker intact; Marrow does not dilute the Red beat).

### c. Voss-Marr — the Rival (read-as-weather, not dialogue)

Gull-Marr's blood; **strongest of Rook's generation, raised to be the next
king** — everything Rook isn't. The Cassius/au Bellona.

- **ch13:** the Tublat tormentor; contempt for the wrong-blood human.
- **ch14–15:** forced to fight side by side vs. the Hesh; grudging respect,
  almost brotherhood.
- **ch16–17 (the break):** Rook challenges **Gull-Marr over Voss's head** and
  takes the crown Voss was raised to inherit; **shames-not-kills** Voss (the
  Kala's kinship lesson), which Voss takes as the deepest insult — "I bled
  beside you and you stole what was mine" (rendered as the air off his still
  body, never as line). On the page in ch17 he is "the held blow at the side"
  that goes back down into him and sets; "I have made a hotter enemy than the
  king, the kind that burns low and long."
- **Part Five:** Voss's faction **hangs back at the trap and blames Rook** for
  the Kala's death and the slaughter (the slavers came for *him*); Rook hauled
  away, **Voss left holding the broken tribe.**
- **Book 2:** Voss has seized the Vorth — the nemesis who won't bow, a war born
  from a Book-1 mercy.

### d. Gull-Marr — the monster-king (Kerchak / the summit)

The reigning king: "a great old slab of a thing," gray through the muzzle,
flat-eyed, who has "weighed every challenge the stone ever threw up and let none
stand." His law is the **law of the strong**: the strong are sure, come in the
rush, and forget to read the ground. **Rook defeats him by pulling the ground
from under him, not by matching strength** (ch17) — drops inside the swing,
takes the back of the knee, "I read where it stands; I pull the ground from
under it." Crucially Rook **does not take the neck / does not lay the brand** —
he takes the *standing*, and **lets the bent king walk alive into the green.**
The key kingship law: *a king of the strength-law is king only so long as the
tribe reads the strength as the thing that keeps them* — once they've watched the
strength go down to a thing too light to pull, the crown moves. "There is no
king in a bent strength."

### e. The Seer — the Vorth elder who senses the blood (the mystery-rhyme)

The oldest Vorth, ash-pale, milk-eyed but still reading; the mentor who **senses
the old blood**. Carries the tribe's recognition of the **Eye-and-Thorn**: lays
a gray finger on the relic-sigil and makes the **means-sound** (a *means* with a
**question** folded in, never finished). In ch17 the finger lifts off the sigil
and lays itself flat **on Rook's chest, over the blood** — reading not the
standing, not the crown, but **the wrongness in the blood itself** (the splits
that closed too fast, the swarm that rings off). "A thing older than the high
stones... the seer has the shape and not the name." Both strain at the same
shape from opposite sides and neither reaches the middle.

> **Mystery-rhyme (lock):** the Seer's read of the **blood** (ch11/17) and the
> slaver-captain's read of the **sigil** (ch21) must point at the **SAME thing**
> — *this blood is worth something / dangerous* — so the reader assembles the
> blood-economy shape with **no explainer**. Book 2 detonates it.

### f. The Pack — the Howlers (the discarded who gather to Rook)

The castoffs who come to the king who values what the strong waste — nucleus of
Book 2's army. Locked members:

- **Grist** — a spared **Skell**, the grind-marks of his own people still on him;
  reads the new order first because the leavings always do. First of the Pack.
- **The Torn One** — an outcast **Vorth she-warrior**, an old wrong healed
  crooked down one side; she does not bow (she is not made to bow); she sets her
  feet and holds a line, which is plainer than bowing.
- **Stub** — a broken castoff the strong stopped reading "because there was
  nothing left in him to take"; drags up on the half-leg and looks up — all he
  has to give, and he gives it.

"These will be the truest of all of them, because they alone know what the order
does to the thing it does not value."

## 5. The Hesh — the Apex

The island's **apex predator/monster**: the largest, most dangerous thing in the
green, the top of the food web above even the Vorth king. It threatens the tribe
in Part Three (ch14–15) and forces Rook and Voss-Marr (and the old male with the
torn ear) to face it together. Rook does not out-muscle it — he defeats/drives
it off through **intelligence + his wrongness** (pain/venom tolerance; wounds
that close too fast as he drags its head up the rock — a beat the Seer later
reads). Over the Hesh going under, Rook lays down his governing law: **"I do not
know what I become if I feed it"** — he refuses to become the hand/the brand —
and keeps that law through his win over the king. The Hesh is **monstrous fauna,
not a people**; it has no society, only appetite — the pure apex against which
the *peoples* (who keep, who drown, who scavenge) are defined.

## 6. The Red, the Bloodless, and the Corruption Ladder (mystery-spec)

> **The single rule: KEEP IT UNSOLVED (the Bloodborne rule).** Never confirm
> what the Red is. Three contradictory theories stand; no one — including Rook —
> knows which is true. The Seer senses it, the slavers' masters want it, the
> Cold fear/hunt it, everyone reacts to the Eye-and-Thorn — **nobody explains
> it.** Book 1 keeps the Red dormant; one faint omen at the pit bottom (ch28),
> pure recognition, **no power-up.**

### The three contradictory theories of the Red (hold all; confirm none)

1. **A fed god** — a blood-**god** the jungle peoples once fed (the blood-temple,
   the Aztec channels).
2. **A bloodline taint** — a **curse** in a degenerate bloodline (Innsmouth /
   Dunwich).
3. **Stolen slain-god's blood** — the **blood of something slain** (a titan /
   dragon / one of the cold ones) that his ancestors drank (Witcher Elder Blood
   / Dune).

### The corruption ladder (the bestiary IS the cost)

A one-way road; the peoples and the Bloodless are rungs on it. **This is the
trilogy's engine and the through-line that makes the monster-peoples cohere:**

| Rung | What | On the page |
|---|---|---|
| **Red** | The rage; a man who burns hot and loses himself in the killing | Dormant in Book 1; one omen at ch28 |
| **Beast** | Pushed further: body warps, the man drowns | **The Mure** (§3b) — the warning hidden in a monster |
| **Husk** | All the way: blood burns out → an empty **cold** thing craving the heat it lost | **The Bloodless** (§6, below) — what he becomes if he leans on the thing that saves him |

So: *the Cold are what Rook becomes if he leans on the thing that keeps him
alive. His own future is the monster; every fight is a step toward it.* (Possible
deep-link; **do NOT confirm on the page.**)

### The Bloodless — "the Cold" (the inverse; the hunters)

- **"Cold" = the absence of blood, NOT winter.** Pale, dry, cold-to-the-touch
  things with **no blood of their own**; they drain the warm and leave **husks**
  (bloodless, neat, no rot, no wound, no smell of death — see the prologue,
  ch00). They **hunt carriers of the Red across the world** — inexorable, always
  coming (It-Follows / Terminator dread).
- **Why they hunt the bloodline (author-truth, never confirmed on page):** the
  Red is the **one thing the Bloodless cannot survive** — Red-blood is fire/
  poison to them, the single force that can end them. So **the Cold hunt the
  bloodline to extinction** (the threat that can kill them), while **men hunt the
  bloodline to own it** (the ultimate weapon/medicine/currency in a world stalked
  by the Cold). Rook is pinned between two predators: the Cold want him *gone*;
  men want him *owned*.
- **They murdered Rook's family for the blood** and still hunt the last of it
  (him). Book 1 keeps this buried; the **prologue (ch00, "Slack Water")** plants
  the Bloodless and the dread with no Rook, no lore, no names — pure GoT
  cold-open.

### The Eye-and-Thorn sigil

The mark of the blood-keeper line. **Eye** (the blood watches / remembers) +
**Thorn** (the blood is drawn / the price) — "the eye looks out around the thing
that pierces it." It lives in Rook from a place older than the island and older
than the hold (it resonates from his drowned-deep where no word lives). It is on
the Wreck-relic he carries bound at his chest; the **Seer reads it as kin-omen**;
the **slaver-captain burns it on the cages** to mean *owned*. To the Cold it is
the brand of the thing that burns them; to men, the mark of priceless blood. The
same mark, two readings — and Rook cannot reconcile them, which is the point.

## 7. The Slavers & the Blood-Trade Across the Water

Humans come **to** Rook: a **slaving expedition lands on Saqarro** to harvest
monsters/curiosities, and unknowingly walks into the death he survived. This
rhymes with the dead expedition in the Wreck (living humans arrive where the
last humans died) and preserves the slave-ship → pits pipeline (they take him
too).

### The camp (ch19)

- **They build where the dead built** — cut the canopy off the old high-water
  flat by the Wreck, open the bare mud, and **do not smell the dying on it.**
  "Men have no nose for the thing that takes them." The **seep is already in
  their water**; they drink it, laugh, and are "already meat." (This seeds the
  free dramatic-irony B-plot: the slavers **sicken from the fever Rook's immune
  to even as they carry him off** — a countdown the reader feels; pays off ch27.)
- **They keep, not kill.** Hooked iron, low cages of wood and lashed iron. They
  take the green's monsters **alive** to carry across the water in a hold, as
  they carried Rook — a caged **Mure** (web-fingers lashed, breath whistling), a
  broken **Skell**, and **men in the cages too** ("men keeping men"). They are
  **blood-traders**, not random raiders — Saqarro's monsters and wrong-bloods are
  the cargo of a blood-economy.
- **Smell-canon:** the camp smells of **the hold** — tar, kept smoke, hot iron,
  and "the close packed reek of bodies held where they cannot move." It puts the
  brand back on the back of Rook's neck.

### Captain Drevan Sool

The still leader. **Not the biggest** — "it is never the biggest" — but "the
others bend toward him without being struck." He has "the still of a thing that
does not have to hurry to what it is going to take" (the same still as the hand
on the hold, as Gull-Marr's slow). He **wears the Eye-and-Thorn** on his breast,
his blade, and burned into the cages. His read of the sigil = **greed AND fear**
(a fortune, and a curse — carriers draw the Cold down on everyone near them),
and it must **rhyme with the Seer's read of the blood** (§4e). Rook learns his
name only late, low, behind the teeth, from the kept.

### Yara — the human captive/translator (the subverted Jane / D'Arnot)

Arrives **late, among the slavers**, as a **coerced translator / another catch**
— a **mirror of Rook's own enslavement, not a damsel.** She fills the **D'Arnot
role** (reads his relic-sigil; begins teaching him human speech) — light but
load-bearing; **romance is a deferred ember for Book 2.** The monster-family,
not she, is the emotional center.

- **Design notes (eval fixes):** commit on naming — name her **earlier in
  narration** or stay fully nameless till Book 2 (don't name her once in ch27
  after six nameless chapters). Give her **one real two-way contact beat before
  the trap** (push the ch21 hand-warning into a genuine exchange where she
  *knows* she's talking to a person and chooses to anyway) — the seed of the
  Book-2 relationship. She is also a live **Book-2 thread** (in the hold / freed
  / lost).

### The blood-trade across the water → the pits (Book 2 handoff)

The known world is **across the water**, stalked by the Cold, where **blood is
currency**. The slave-ship → **the pits**, the bottom of a **blood-city** built
on the blood-trade (vertical pyramid: the bled at the bottom, the blood-courts
at the top). The arena bleeds wrong-bloods to see what their blood *does*. The
blood-soaked ground there **wakes the Red** (it grows where blood is spilled),
which wakes **ancestral memory**, which is where Rook's heritage detonates and
his murdered family returns (Book 2). The Vorth/Mure/Skell harvested by slavers
also land there — "unite the monster-clans" becomes **unite the bled.**

## 8. Design Note — The Frozen Four Tribes → Book 2's Blood-City Factions

The superseded doc `docs/world/four-tribes.md` (the four *frozen* Blackjaw
tribes — **Khar Vane**, **Salt Veyr**, **Fen-Kin**, **Hollow Antler**) is
**retired from Book 1** (wrong climate, wrong magic, pre-blood). But its
**political-machine thinking is gold and carries FORWARD** as the design DNA for
**Book 2's blood-city factions.** The value was never the tundra dressing — it
was the **"unstable machine"**: four powers, each with a *rational, non-evil*
reason to exist, fight, bargain, and **misread the protagonist differently**, so
that one event (Rook's survival / his blood) gives each faction a *different
nightmare*. Carry that engine into the blood-city.

**The faction-logic to carry forward (climate-stripped, blood-mapped):**

| Frozen tribe | The portable engine (faction logic) | Maps forward to a Book-2 blood-city faction |
|---|---|---|
| **Salt Veyr** (traders) | **Stability/commerce faction** — needs the market and routes intact; fears unification under a war-power because war breaks trade. Reads Rook as a *market shock*. | The **blood-merchants / brokers** of the blood-economy — they price the wrong-blood, fear the Red destabilizing the trade more than they fear the Cold. |
| **Hollow Antler** (priesthood) | **Interpretive-authority faction** — rules not by numbers but by *saying what events mean*; power depends on controlling omen/taboo/punishment. Needs to **own the meaning** of Rook's survival before a rival names it first. | The **blood-cult / blood-courts' priesthood** — the keepers of what the Red "means," the tithe-rites, the temple of the city. Threatened when the wrong-blood's reality outruns their interpretation. |
| **Fen-Kin** (old-blood lineage) | **Bloodline-control faction** — the genealogists who believe *some blood remembers before the mind does*; the only ones who may know what his blood *is*, which makes them useful and dangerous; must decide if he's a lost branch, a curse, or a weapon to end. | The **bloodline-houses / blood-keeper line** — those who hunt or claim carriers of the Red; the in-city echo of the hunted-bloodline through-line; may hold a fragment of who Rook's house was. |
| **Khar Vane** (cannibal war tribe, **pit-keepers**) | **Sacred-conquest / consumption faction** — ritual consumption that carries courage/memory/debt; pit-keepers who want sacred *proof* to break the others; believe after the pit that Rook *belongs to them or they to him*. | The **arena-masters / the consumers at the top** — those who run the pits, fight and bleed wrong-bloods, and consume the Red. The pit-city's apex predators of the blood-trade. **Direct line: their pit = the pit Book 1's cliffhanger lands in.** |

**The portable structural beats** (from the frozen doc's "First Political
Sequence" — reusable wholesale as the shape of Rook's blood-city rise):

1. A **commerce** faction finds the prize first and **hides** it (market shock).
2. An **interpretive** faction seizes him during a **jurisdiction dispute** and
   tries to fix his meaning.
3. A **bloodline** faction recognizes old-blood markers and argues against
   careless destruction (he may be *useful*, or *theirs*).
4. A **conquest/pit** faction demands the **arena rite**.
5. He's thrown into the pit as **compromise, punishment, and test.**
6. **The Red wakes** (Book 2's detonation).
7. The conquest faction **claims the result** before the interpreters can
   explain it away.

**Carry-forward keepers (faction names/characters available as a Book-2
quarry):** the frozen doc's named characters (Maren Salt-Hand, Tovek Reed-Eye,
Vessa, Old Brannoc, Asha Fen, Mother Vey, Harvek Bone-Mouth, Solek the Thin,
etc.) are **NOT canon** but are a usable name/archetype bank when the Book-2
blood-city factions are built out. **Note one collision:** the frozen doc used a
"**Yara Khar**" (a pit-singer) — Book 1 now owns **Yara** as the human
captive/translator (§7); the two are unrelated and the frozen "Yara Khar" should
be renamed if drawn from the quarry.

> **Net:** Book 1 = the **island** and the **found family** (Vorth/Mure/Skell,
> the Kala, the crown, the trap). Book 2 = the **blood-city** and the **four-
> faction unstable machine** above, where Rook's blood gives each faction a
> different nightmare and his rise runs the seven-beat sequence from the bottom
> of the pit upward.
