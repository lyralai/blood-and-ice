## 1. Proposed Family Name and Lineage

Current bibles use "old northern blood" and "old-blood vessel" without a specific house name. This new canon requires naming the ruling line for durability.

**Recommendation:** Introduce the family name as **the Rauth** (or "House Rauth", "the Rauth line", "the Rauth kings").

- Northern / cold-feeling without being on-the-nose.
- Distinct from tribe names (Salt Veyr, Hollow Antler, Fen-Kin, Khar Vane).
- Allows "Rauth blood", "the last Rauth", "Rauth's son".
- Ties naturally to ice-blood: "Rauth" can evoke "wrath" + "rauth" (old northern root for counsel/red or counsel/reckoning) while remaining original.
- The tribes know the name because they were subjects; after the rebellions and isolation, speaking it carries weight (fear, resentment, old loyalty, or calculated use).

Alternative shortlist if "Rauth" does not land: "the Skarn", "the Vinter", "the Hald", "the Tharn". "Rauth" is the strongest current suggestion for voice fit.

**Integration into Dual Ancestry:**
- Father's line = Ice-Blood dominant (the "cold ones" inheritance the user referenced). The Rauth were the preservers, the name-holders, the ones who could endure the northern cold and hold the old blood under pressure.
- The rebellions were not only political (desire for independence) but also resistance to the Rauth monopoly on the blood-god sites and rites. The tribes wanted the power without the Rauth vessel.
- Rook's father put down the last rebellion using a combination of ice-blood discipline + controlled (or not) Wound-Guest force. This made him both feared as a repressor and mythologized as the last strong king.

## 2. The 30-Year Isolation as Severing + Magical Punishment

The isolation is not just political exile from the wider world. It is a deliberate severing of the blood rites.

**Political Layer (visible to all):**
- After the second rebellion (put down by Rook's father), the victorious side (or the remaining Rauth loyalists + certain tribe elders) imposed 30 years of isolation as punishment.
- No outside ships, no new iron, limited inter-island movement in the early years, heavy penalties for signaling or sending word.
- The tribes were forced to turn inward and starve the old power structures.

**Ritual / Magical Layer (tied to dual heritage and White Pit):**
- The blood gods (or the force the Wound-Guest embodies) are strongest on the Blackjaw Isles, particularly at the White Pit.
- The Rauth line acted as the traditional "vessels" or "keys" that kept the rites stable. Their rule was the channel.
- The rebellions + the isolation were an attempt to close that channel: starve the gods of named vessels, break the lineage, let the old blood fade.
- For 30 years the force has been "sleeping" or weakened. The tribes still perform reduced rites (Hollow Antler containment, Khar pit-keeping) but without a true Rauth vessel the power does not fully wake or travel the way it once did.
- Rook landing + participating in the White Pit ceremony (the traditional test/ sacrifice on the strongest site) is the first time in a generation that a living Rauth blood has entered the pit under the old conditions (fear, confinement, blood loss, near-death, public witnessing by all four tribes).
- This is the "magic coming back" moment the user likes: the isolation's severing is broken by the return of the specific bloodline the force was bound to. Ice-blood (Rauth endurance + name-holding) provides the grip; the blood-god force floods in because the old channel is re-opened.

This makes Rook not a random feral boy who gets power. He is the last heir of the line the tribes overthrew, returning to the exact site where his ancestors drew power, at the exact moment the punishment period is still active.

## 3. How This Strengthens the Dual-Heritage Arc (ch1-36 + Future)

**Pre-Pit / Wild-Boy (ch1-3 and part-01 novella):**
- Rook does not know (or only half-remembers) the family name at first. The shipwreck and years alone have stripped titles.
- Ice-blood expressions are subtle and survival-based: cold endurance, animal attention, flashes of "old knowing" when he holds the antler pole or stands on the black shore.
- He is literally the last of the line, living in the ruins of the punishment he did not cause. This adds tragic weight to the "alone on the island" phase.

**White Pit (ch8-10):**
- The ceremony is not just a Khar demand or Hollow containment. It is the traditional Rauth test that the tribes remember from before the rebellions.
- When Rook goes into the pit, the isolation's severing + the return of Rauth blood + the ritual conditions cause the blood-god force to recognize the old vessel and re-ignite.
- The "laughter under pain" and violent healing are the force remembering how it used to ride the Rauth line.
- Public myth after emergence is double-edged: some see the return of the old kings' power (Khar weapon-worship); others see the return of the repressor line (Hollow fear, Salt calculation).

**Post-Crown / Systems Phase (ch21-36 + Packet 5/6):**
- The tribes knowing the name creates constant political friction.
  - Khar: "The Rauth has returned. The pit boy is the old blood. We should feed it."
  - Hollow Antler (Vessa / Mother Harl / Naim): Inner law and three-name doctrine become weapons because the "blood name spoken by the vessel" now carries the weight of the old ruling line. Naim's silences (ch32/36) are even more loaded — he is choosing not to give the old name power.
  - Fen-Kin (Asha): The old-blood check and wrapped spear caller training are not neutral. Asha recognizes the Rauth lineage that made the pit vessel viable in the first place. The caller is partly an old Rauth tool repurposed.
  - Salt (Maren): Practical. They remember the Rauth as the ones who could enforce the old stores and roads. Maren's "thin trust" root bundle and later guarantees are calculations about whether the last Rauth can be turned into a useful counted thing rather than a returned tyrant.
- Small tribes like Reed-Bend (ch32): They calculate between two hungers — the old Rauth name (which once protected or ruled them) vs the immediate local power (Dremm/Half-Ear faction taking eel runs).
- Guest learning: The Wound-Guest now has access to "Rauth" as a shape. Names have bellies becomes more dangerous because the family name itself is a historical claim the force can learn to wear.
- Naim inner law (ch30): The three-name rule (dead + living non-Antler witness + blood name spoken by the vessel) is revealed in private because Naim knows Rook is Rauth. Speaking the family name in the right ritual context would close (or re-open) paths in ways that affect the whole island.

**Long Arc Implication:**
The story is no longer "feral boy gains power." It is "last heir of the overthrown line returns during the tail end of the punishment isolation, re-ignites the old blood force, and must decide whether to become the new version of his father's repression or build something that does not feed only the Wound-Guest."

Every small loop, road-sworn, cradle tenth, and "the road's due" becomes a test of whether the Rauth name can be remade into law that the blood-god force has not yet learned to exploit.
