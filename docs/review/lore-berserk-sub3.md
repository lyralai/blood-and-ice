## Core Concept

The Wound-Guest is Rook's name for the blood-god force (called Below-Eater by Khar Vane, Red Listener by Hollow Antler). It is *not* simple rage or a berserker rage power. It is an expansive, consuming, learning presence that uses Rook's ice-blood inheritance as a superior "vessel" (White Pit unlock makes the grip sustainable rather than immediately self-destructive — which is worse for the boy).

**Bloody-Nine Adaptation (visceral, not clean):**  
- Uncontrollable laughter rising from the ribs under mortal pain (alien joy, not triumph).  
- Hyper-violent clarity: precise, efficient disabling or slaughter rather than wild flailing (ice-blood cold precision twists the blood-hunger into something sustained and strategic).  
- Total or near-total loss of self ("I" recedes; actions feel borrowed, watched, or performed by something older). Second breath below his own. Red pressure that feels like another set of lungs.  
- Sensory sharpening (bloodline smell, movement prediction, pain as fuel) mixed with intrusive hunger and strategic images that are not his.  
- It does not "power up" cleanly. Every manifestation educates the Guest, erodes Rook's ownership of his name/story, and makes future holds costlier. Witnesses prefer or fear the monster more than the boy. Restraint itself becomes a lesson the thing files away ("restraint is waste"; "law is a slower, larger meal").

It arrives through the body first (red heat from wounds, iron taste, pressure under ribs, laughter not chosen), then through appetite and images, then (later) almost-words and patient strategy. Early: mostly blind hunger. Later (post-ch26): articulate bargainer that waits inside calls, crowns, and systems.

## Dual Blood + White Pit Grounding

From magic.md and protagonist.md:  
- **Ice-blood** (northern inheritance): conservative, preserves, remembers, binds to lineage/place. Gives endurance in cold, resistance to panic, ability to hold a name under pain, animal attention, slow healing. Makes Rook a "better vessel" — the blood-god force can grip without burning through him immediately.  
- **Blood-god force** (Wound-Guest): expansive, consumes, learns by injury. Violent healing, battle-state possession, laughter under pain, borrowed strength, intrusive hunger, alien strategic clarity, smell-memory of bloodlines, temporary loss of fear.  

White Pit (ch9 full awakening): ritual fear + confinement + blood loss + near death. Most sacrifices die or break. Rook returns changed because ice-blood gives the force purchase. The pit does not give back a boy; it gives back a door. This is the root "blood monster" unlock — sustainable possession rather than one-shot frenzy.

The mix is unstable and educational for the Guest. Ice-blood's cold clarity lets the violence be precise and the hunger strategic. This is not a gift; it is why the thing gets smarter faster than a pure berserker would.

## Manifestation Mechanics (visceral, physical, costly)

**Sensory / Bodily Onset (body-first, per magic.md rule):**  
- Red pressure behind the eyes, climbing the ribs like a closing hand or second breath. Warmth spreading from wounds (even old ones) that feels like borrowed blood.  
- Iron taste flooding the mouth (self-bite or internal). Sweat at hairline in cold. Red at the edges of vision "like water rising under ice" or "second eyes." Voice rough or not fully his.  
- Laughter that is not chosen — rising from the ribs under pain, alien and pleased. It can be low/internal or public. Witnesses hear it as wrong.  
- Loss of ordinary fear + hyper-clarity: pain narrows into cold precision (ice-blood twist). Smell of bloodlines and fear. Movement prediction. Efficient, strategic violence rather than blind frenzy (later stages).  
- "I" recedes. Actions feel watched or performed through him. Intrusive images (clean kill lines, debt bowl poured across thresholds so tallies carry the mark, flats closed in a fist until nothing moves, coughing children as the ones who will "feed the story"). Almost-words or full pressure-phrases ("Kill caller," "Door," "This is not waste," "Fear is the store that does not freeze," "Please").  

**Physical Tells / After-Effects (anchors and costs visible):**  
- Cheek or tongue bite to draw own blood and assert "mine" (ch26, ch30).  
- Hand shaking on wrapped spear long after pressure passes (ch36, ch35).  
- Voice strain, knees bending toward spring, red vision edges taking longer to clear.  
- Post-recession: memory gaps (early pit), intrusive pre-tribal memories (ch21: red hands on white ice, circle of kneeling men with open mouths, boy with cut throat laughing through another mouth), changed appetite (raw meat or blood rites), difficulty feeling ordinary fear or tenderness.  

**Witness Layer (public meaning shift, per protagonist.md and cost-ledger):**  
- Posture/voice pressure changes. Khar Vane see the returned pit-thing and prefer it for deterrence. Salt/Fen see the myth grow around the worst part of him. Small tribes arrive second (after Vessa's salt or Half-Ear's laugh) and the name feels heavier/thinner.  
- Yara's single low note or silence carries the shape. Asha steps back. Cairn's hand stays on the bone blade.  

**Not a Clean Power-Up:** The clarity and strength come with strategic hunger that learns. It offers "solutions" (debt bowl as traveling mark, tribute via fear, small loops pulled tighter) that feed the story larger and slower. Restraint educates it on what the boy values. Every hold is a lesson.
