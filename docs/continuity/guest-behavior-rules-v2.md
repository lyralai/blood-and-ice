# Guest Behavior Rules v2 — The Red Articulation & Learning Ceiling

**Purpose**: Codify the safe escalation ladder for the Red (Blood-God Force / Below-Eater / Red Listener). Preserve its core strength as a *strategic learner of systems* (stores, names, debts, small loops, callers, crowns, due claims) while strictly enforcing the articulation ceiling. The Guest must remain dangerous, bodily, expensive, and only *almost* articulate for the majority of Book One.

**Core Principle (unchanged from magic.md + protagonist.md)**: 
The Red communicates through appetite, images, pressure, sudden knowing, and strategic hunger. It learns by injury, restraint, and witnessed victories. It does **not** speak in neat sentences early. Every escalation in clarity must be earned through visible physical and political cost to Rook, and must leave the reader feeling the thing is becoming a better ruler *because* the boy is trying to hold law.

## Safe Escalation Ladder (Book One Ceiling)

**Level 0 — Pre-Pit / Reflex (ch1-8)**: 
- Heat/pressure behind eyes or under ribs.
- Sharpened senses, intrusive impulse, laughter not his.
- No images or words. Pure bodily override.

**Level 1 — Early Post-Pit (ch9-20)**: 
- Violent healing, loss of ordinary fear, smell-memory of bloodlines.
- Intrusive images (pre-tribal or alien).
- Single alien certainties ("hunger", "kill", "door" as raw shape).
- Physical cost mandatory and visible (memory gaps, changed posture/voice, appetite spikes, public dread).

**Level 2 — Post-Crown Learning Phase (ch21-36 target ceiling)**: 
**Allowed**:
- Clear but non-verbal images (teeth on stone, debt bowl poured across threshold, flats closed in a fist, coughing children as easy lift, inlet under Khar hands).
- Single almost-words or tasted concepts ("Store" vs food, "Door", names tasted/repeated like "Rook", "small loop", "road's due").
- Strategic hunger *expressed as images + pressure* (the littlest will feed the story; fear is the store that does not freeze; name the due and the small send).
- Low internal reactions: pleased warmth, low laugh-like feeling, patient settling, "noted the shape for later".
- Boredom/disinterest in clean administration (wound care, baskets, counting, names) — this is a feature, not a bug. It forces Rook to handle systems alone.

**Strictly Limited / Borderline (use sparingly, always with heavy cost)**:
- Short, broken almost-phrases under extreme pressure (e.g. "Please" as plea during public challenge; "Kill caller" as shape/certainty).
- Conceptual reframes that are still image-distorted ("Names have bellies"; "the counters lie with numbers").

**Forbidden until explicit later pivot (mid-Book One crisis or deliberate "it has learned too much" beat)**:
- Full grammatical sentences in the Guest's "voice".
- Clean strategic plans or philosophical statements ("This is not waste. Fear is the store... Tribute before the ice takes the name.").
- Bargaining language or meta-understanding of tools ("Let the spear-mother name the bend as yours in blood. I will close the hands..."; "the wrapped call is not only a leash but a door"; pushing name-repetition back at the caller).
- Relational pleas or "almost speech" that feels like a second personality negotiating.
- Administrative solutions offered as if the Guest understands governance (starve own promises, cut weak claims first, etc.) without heavy Rook filtering or image corruption.

## Mandatory Cost & Visibility Rules (enforce in every scene)

1. **Physical toll always shown**: Iron taste flooding mouth, sweat at hairline (even in cold), red at edges of vision like second eyes or rising water, voice strain/cracking, hand shaking on spear/band after recession, biting cheek/tongue/lip until blood, knees bending toward spring, pressure climbing ribs like closing hand or ice.
2. **Active anchoring required for Rook**: Self-name ("Rook", "Rook who came back", "Rook of no shore", "Rook who holds the small loop") spoken internally or aloud. Counting witnesses/exits aloud. Wrapped spear grip (red thread bite). Bone band. Public naming of the law term being defended.
3. **Witness & political cost**: Every use or near-use must visibly cost follower loyalty (especially young Khar / Dremm knot), widen the gap between boy and myth, or teach external checks (Asha, Cairn, Maren, Mother Harl) that the education is double-edged.
4. **Learning is shown, not told**: Guest "files" or "notes the shape" of systems (store vs food, small loop, road's due, debt bowl as tool, caller rhythm as door) via images + low internal pleasure/laugh + later behavior. Never via clean exposition from the Guest.
5. **Boredom with systems is protective**: When the Guest disengages from clean politics/admin (ch28), use it to highlight Rook's isolation in the "system" phase. Do not let the Guest suddenly become interested in counting unless it is learning to weaponize the count (still via images).

## Trigger & Restraint Guidelines

- Guest rises hardest on: direct threat to vessel, public dominance challenges, named small/weak prey, new systems being built (stores, loops, due, callers), restraint that denies immediate meat.
- Restraint and training (wrapped spear, self-name anchors, Fen caller) are the primary educators. Every successful hold teaches the Guest a new shape it can later wait inside or turn.
- Do not trigger on convenience. If Rook can call it easily, tension collapses.
- Post-use recession must leave visible after-effects (shaking, longer red clearance, public observers noting distance or readiness in Asha/Cairn).

## "Almost Articulate" Guardrails for ch21-36 (and next packets)

- Prefer: Image + bodily pressure + one tainted concept.
- Acceptable rare escalation: One broken almost-phrase only when anchor is visibly failing and physical cost is extreme.
- Never: Let the Guest speak a full strategic sentence that Rook then "resists." The resistance must happen *inside* the image pressure.
- When Guest offers a "plan," it must arrive corrupted by hunger (the littlest will feed the story; their small mouths will make the crown strong) and be immediately countered by Rook's physical anchoring + political cost awareness.
- The learning ceiling is the point of the story: the better Rook builds law and systems to contain the thing, the more sophisticated a monster he is teaching.

## Later Series Escalation (post-Book One, for roadmap only)

Full sentences, relational speech, clean administrative hunger, and the Guest actively using crown tools as its own voice are reserved for the crisis where the boy-king problem peaks — when the island (or Rook) has given the thing enough victories that it no longer needs full takeover to rule through the systems the boy built.
