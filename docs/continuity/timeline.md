# Timeline

## Book One

### Chapter 1: Black Shore

- Rook wakes on the black shore after the sea throws him back.
- He scavenges from bodies and steals a strip of dried fish.
- Maren Salt-Hand and Salt Veyr scouts find him.
- Rook wounds Senn with a bone toggle and steals his knife.
- The red heat under Rook's ribs stirs for the first time on page.
- **Novella pass (2026-06-13):** Subtle ice-blood planting — shore/stones recognize returning blood; black stones "had been waiting longer than the wreck"; antler pole sound = something old that learned to wait; heat waited longer than the wreck, cold tried to starve it; ending stones know the shape of the blood, "last of something old and broken had come home"; cold part remembers how to hold a name under pain. Name remains latent. No new plot events.

### Chapter 2: Hunger Law

- Salt Veyr brings Rook to the cove camp.
- Rook observes knot-debt, fish stores, and the antler warning pole.
- Maren stages a simple food/blood/debt lesson.
- Rook breaks the bone toggle instead of keeping or returning it.
- Senn gives up his knife into Maren's keeping; a debt knot is tied.
- **Novella pass (2026-06-13):** Subtle ice-blood/Rauth planting — bone on pole: "The last of something had touched what the dead had left to mark the shore. My blood answered before I knew why I should fear the answer"; memory of names feeding the dead; debt scene: "Both knew the knots the dead had used to keep names fed when the isolation had tried to starve the line." Cold vs red tension. Name latent.

### Chapter 3: First Blood In The Trees

- Rook is taken inland to prove whether he can track or only steal.
- Salt Veyr scouts are ambushed by a Hollow Antler boundary party.
- Rook is wounded and partially enters the red-state.
- He saves Senn by attacking through pain.
- Survivors now have enough rumor to make him valuable and dangerous.
- **Novella pass (2026-06-13):** Subtle planting — white bark claim "The claim was older than the snares. The bones on the shore pole had waited for the blood that carried the old line to return and answer it." Cold bones recognize older claim; red likes it. Ice-blood holds red from finishing. Name latent.

### Chapter 4: Salt Knots

- Maren translates the ambush into leverage.
- Rook learns that debt can protect him and bind him at once.
- Tovek Reed-Eye is introduced as interpreter and information broker.
- Salt Veyr prepares to move Rook before Hollow Antler claims him.

### Chapter 5: Antlers In Fog

- Hollow Antler intercepts Salt Veyr under ritual law.
- Vessa names Rook as blood under wrong weather.
- The priesthood claims jurisdiction through omen and wound.
- Maren cannot fight without risking trade routes.
- Rook is taken toward the high fog woods.

### Chapter 6: Fen Names

- Fen-Kin intervenes before the ritual claim is sealed.
- Asha Fen recognizes old-blood signs in Rook.
- Cairn marks Rook as rival and possible catastrophe.
- Fen-Kin argues that careless sacrifice may wake a debt older than the tribes.
- The dispute widens from local punishment into island politics.

### Chapter 7: Khar Vane Demand

- Harvek Bone-Mouth arrives under horn and oath.
- Yara Khar reads the scene as future song.
- Harvek reframes Rook as test rather than prisoner.
- The White Pit becomes the compromise every faction can justify.
- Rook understands that fairness can be another mask for killing.

### Chapter 8: The Descent

- Rook is washed, cut, bound, and given a temporary death-name.
- Yara explains enough of Khar Vane law to make the rite coherent and terrifying.

### Chapter 9: Below Hunger
