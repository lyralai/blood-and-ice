# Cost Ledger

Track every meaningful Wound-Guest event here.

## Rule

No major use of the red-state is free. The cost may be physical, social, political, spiritual, or psychological, but it must alter the story.

## Event 1: Black Shore Stirring

**Chapter:** 1

**Trigger:** Rook's side wound opens while he is cornered by Salt Veyr scouts.

**Expression:** sharpened senses, red heat under ribs, intrusive impulse to attack again.

**Immediate benefit:** Rook gets Senn's knife and stops the scouts from treating him as easy shore-meat.

**Cost:** Maren sees enough to classify him as abnormal. Rook's first relationship with Salt Veyr begins in debt and fear.

## Event 2: Antler Pole Stillness

**Chapter:** 2

**Trigger:** Rook touches a weathered finger bone tied to an antler warning pole.

**Expression:** the red heat goes still; Rook receives a flash of old name-memory.

**Immediate benefit:** none practical.

**Cost:** Maren notices he felt something. Rook becomes more than a violent stray in her risk calculation.

## Event 3: Trees Ambush Partial Red-State

**Chapter:** 3

**Trigger:** Rook is cut and Senn is nearly taken by a Hollow Antler boundary party.

**Expression:** pain narrows into clarity; smell and movement sharpen; Rook attacks through injury.

**Immediate benefit:** Senn survives and Salt Veyr witnesses Rook's usefulness.

**Cost:** the story of Rook changes from dangerous boy to possible omen. Hollow Antler gets ritual grounds to claim him.

## Event 4: White Pit Full Awakening

**Chapter:** 9

**Trigger:** ritual fear, confinement, blood loss, and near death in the White Pit.

**Expression:** full takeover; red sensory clarity; loss of ordinary fear; violent healing; laughter and strategy that are not Rook's.

**Immediate benefit:** Rook survives the pit and defeats the thing/prisoner below.

**Cost:** memory gap, public myth, physical change, and Khar Vane witnesses who prefer the returned thing to the boy.

## Event 5: Return Witnessing

**Chapter:** 10

**Trigger:** Rook emerges wounded and alive in front of all four tribal powers.

**Expression:** the Wound-Guest recedes but leaves altered posture, voice pressure, appetite, and public dread.

**Immediate benefit:** Khar Vane claims him as sacred proof, preventing Hollow Antler from quietly killing or containing him.

**Cost:** Rook loses ownership of his own survival story. The island begins building a myth around the worst part of him.

## Event 6: Fen Challenge Refusal

**Chapter:** 15

**Trigger:** Cairn tests Rook's body ownership while Rook is still wounded and politically exposed.

**Expression:** the Wound-Guest offers clean killing lines and urges Rook to answer challenge with throat-opening efficiency.

**Immediate benefit:** Rook deliberately yields before the red-state can define the scene.

**Cost:** Dremm and other Khar Vane followers see restraint as betrayal. The Wound-Guest also learns the shape of restraint, making future refusals less simple.

## Event 7: Feast Bowl Blood

**Chapter:** 17

**Trigger:** Dremm strikes Rook during the sacramental feast dispute.

**Expression:** the Wound-Guest rises with a direct kill impulse, but Rook spits blood into the ash/debt bowl and redirects the moment into law.

**Immediate benefit:** Rook avoids killing Dremm and turns appetite into a logistics punishment.

**Cost:** Rook accidentally creates a new rite-image: living blood over enemy debt. Yara and Khar Vane now have another symbol to spread.

## Event 8: Second Pit Name-Holding

**Chapter:** 18

**Trigger:** Vessa repeatedly asks what Rook is during the four-tribe witnessed hollow rite.

**Expression:** blood-memory visions, pressure under the ribs, and the Wound-Guest pushing to answer as hunger.

**Immediate benefit:** Rook holds his mouth shut until dawn and answers only with his own name.

**Cost:** the Wound-Guest becomes more articulate under restraint, and Vessa learns which questions reach the boy.

## Event 9: Blood Snow Half-Red

**Chapter:** 19

**Trigger:** Hollow Antler arrows wound Rook and Dremm breaks formation, threatening the battle plan.

**Expression:** Rook lets the red-state rise halfway: speed, pain tolerance, tactical violence, and efficient disabling.

**Immediate benefit:** Rook breaks the archer line without full slaughter and preserves the Salt road crossing.

**Cost:** every mercy requires active resistance against the Wound-Guest. Dremm's disobedient killing proves Rook cannot yet command the myth he has become.

## Event 10: Blood-Crown Terms

**Chapter:** 20

**Trigger:** Khar Vane demands a war-crown around Rook after Blood Snow and Dremm's mutilated return.

**Expression:** the Wound-Guest rises at the crown rite as if hearing a door open.

**Immediate benefit:** Rook binds the crown with terms: the Wound-Guest is not king, and Fen old-blood can call or condemn him if he goes red and does not return.

**Cost:** the Wound-Guest becomes stronger, quieter, and more interested in law. Leadership itself becomes the next pit.
