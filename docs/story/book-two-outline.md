# BOOK TWO: THE RED AND THE BLED — Story Outline

> **Status:** Working outline (good enough to write from, not locked stone).
> **Series:** "Ice and Blood," Book 2 of 3. Spine: Boy → Beast → Great Man.
> **POV:** Rook, first-person present (continuous with Book 1).
> **Length target:** ~95–110k words · ~31 chapters · five parts.
> **Picks up from:** Book 1, ch28 ("The Pit") — Rook and Marrow thrown into the
> arena-pit of a vertical blood-city; the Kala dead; the crown and island lost;
> Voss-Marr left holding the broken Vorth; the Red still dormant, omen only.
>
> **Canon governors (do not violate — see `docs/world/the-red-and-the-bloodless.md`):**
> the Red stays UNSOLVED (three theories, no confirmation); spilled blood is the
> throttle; every use costs something measurable; the corruption ladder is one-way
> (Red → Beast/Mure → Husk/Bloodless) and Rook's own future is the monster;
> articulacy in the Red is the corruption signal, not progress; "Cold" = absence
> of blood, never winter; coldness/the still place/the count is his armor and
> human anchors are his ballast. **Title note:** Book 2 brings the Red *awake* and
> brings the Cold to the *walls* — the "Ice" of the series surfaces here as the
> Bloodless pressing in, not as climate.

## 1. PREMISE & ENGINE

**Premise.** Dropped to the bottom of a city built on blood, the wrong-blooded
boy who would not die finds the one place on earth where his blood finally
*wakes* — and learns, with his murdered family's memory pouring back through it,
that he is the thing the whole vertical machine is built to harvest, the thing
its enemy is built to hunt, and the only thing that can hold both off. He climbs
from the bled at the bottom toward the blood-courts at the top, turning the
city's appetite into law one debt at a time — while the Red learns to wear him,
the factions each make a different weapon of his blood, and out past the walls
the Cold the tithe was built to hold begins, for the first time in living memory,
to come.

**The engine (carried from Book 1, scaled up).** *Everyone has a different USE
for the shape of him,* and no two powers can let a third own him. He rises not by
force but by playing the factions' fears against each other — the same machine
that ran the Vorth tribe, now a city. Three pressures run under every chapter and
never resolve:

1. **The rise** (Red-Rising): bottom of the pit → the bled → the arena legend →
   the leashed crown of the under-city → the failing top. *Unite the clans*
   becomes **unite the bled.**
2. **The leash** (the first-restraint / crown-with-terms mechanic from the
   frozen quarry, ported intact): every scrap of power he takes comes with a
   bite. The crown answers to law before appetite; an outside hand holds the
   kill-switch; restraint reads as betrayal to his own base.
3. **The cost** (the corruption ladder): the Red is the only thing that wins the
   pit, and every win down it is a step toward becoming the Cold. The better it
   saves him, the more everyone prefers the monster to the boy — and the more
   articulate, strategic, and *administrative* the thing in his blood becomes.
   He does not win the peace with the Red. He wins it with the still place, the
   count, and the people he keeps. The Red only ever wins the fight.

**The horror shift (the freshest layer, mined from the frozen Part 6).** Book 2
moves the dread from *cannibal-hunger* to **the cold logic of letting the weak
die "usefully."** As Rook learns to rule the under-city by counting — who eats,
who guards, who is bled first — the Red **learns to count with him.** It stops
wanting blood and starts wanting the clean administrative answer: choose who the
Cold takes first, no pit, no arena, just the ledger. Training the thing to be
restrained is teaching it how to wear the crown of the feeding apparatus. That is
the road to the husk, walked in daylight, in a counting-house.

**One-line through-line.** *A blood-city keeps the Cold at bay by tithing blood
it does not understand; the boy is the priceless carrier every faction needs
differently; he tries to turn the tithe from appetite into law before his own
rising power — and the thing in his blood — teaches the city, and him, how to
wear the crown of the feeding apparatus, just as the tithe starts to fail and the
Cold starts to come.*

## 2. THE BLOOD-CITY (setting)

Across the water from Saqarro: **Vgrael** *(working name)* — not a tribe-ground
but a **vertical city built into a drowned-coast sinkhole**, a tiered shaft of
old stone and lashed timber descending from open sky at the rim to lightless
sumps at the bottom. The whole pyramid is organized around one fact it half
believes and never proves: **the tithe keeps the Cold from the walls.**

- **The bottom — the bled.** Sumps, cages, bleeding-houses. Slaves, captured
  island-monsters (Vorth, Mure, Skell hauled across the water), and *wrong-bloods*
  — anyone whose blood does something — milked low after low and thrown to the
  arena when they fail to die fast enough. This is where ch28 dropped him.
- **The middle — the working tiers.** Brokers' counting-galleries, broker-routes
  and tithe-runs, the bleeding-houses' books, the warrens of the freed-but-owing.
  The administrative body of the city. This is the ground Rook actually conquers —
  not with teeth, with the count.
- **The top — the blood-courts.** The temple of the Red and the broker-houses
  that hoard, price, and consume the tithe. They never come down. They are fed.
- **The arena (the throat).** A bled-floor cut into the middle tiers where
  wrong-bloods are fought and bled *to see what their blood does* — spectacle,
  harvest, and research at once. The earth of it is black with centuries of
  killing. It is the throttle that wakes the Red, and the stage on which Rook
  becomes a legend he then has to survive being.
- **The wall & the rim.** Out past the rim, the cold latitudes where the Cold
  walks. The tithe-doctrine says the bleeding is what keeps the rim warm. Whether
  that is true, exaggerated, or a broker-priest fiction is the central tension —
  and Book 2 is the season the question stops being academic.

**The Eye-and-Thorn** is carved everywhere here — on the tithe-stones, the
cages, the court lintels — because this whole economy is the sigil made into an
institution: **Eye** (the blood watches/remembers) + **Thorn** (the blood is
drawn / the price / the tithe). Rook arrives branded with the same mark the city
worships, and no one can agree what that means.

## 3. THE FACTION MAP (the four-power unstable machine)

Converted from the frozen four tribes per `saqarro-and-the-peoples.md` §8 — keep
the *function*, drop the frozen names. Each faction is a rational survival
strategy; each misreads Rook differently; each one's best case is another's
nightmare. He survives in the gap between their fears.

| Faction | Function (the portable engine) | Their nightmare about him | Frozen quarry roles re-cast |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Arena-Masters / the Red Priesthood-of-the-pit** (war/consumption) | Apex predators who run the pits, bleed wrong-bloods, and *consume* the Red; want sacred proof to break rivals; believe that after the pit he "belongs to them or they to him." **Their pit is the one ch28 dropped him into.** | A living vessel makes their rite *true* — but a vessel more sacred than the masters means the mob obeys appetite, not them. | **Harvek-function:** the old patron-predator who counts meat and debt over miracles — first dangerous sponsor. **Yara-Khar-function** (RENAMED — Book 1 owns "Yara"): the pit-singer / **myth-broadcaster** who *names* the event for a writing-less crowd before anyone can object. **Dremm-function:** the dangerous devotee who loves the monster first. |
| **The Blood-Brokers** (commerce/stability) | They *price* wrong-blood, run the tithe-routes and the bleeding-houses; fear the Red destabilizing the trade more than they fear the Cold; read him as a *market shock*. | If the war-faction owns the priceless carrier, every price in the city resets and the routes are theirs. | **Maren-function:** the logistics realist who sees through sacred theater — *who eats, who guards, who profits from panic*; bargains without worship; arrives with ledgers and joint-stores; becomes indispensable. |
| **The Blood-Courts / Temple-of-the-Red** (interpretive authority) | Keepers of what the Red *means* — the tithe-doctrine, the rites, the "Cold kept at bay." Power is the control of meaning. Threatened the instant his reality outruns their interpretation. | A miracle they cannot name becomes a rival faith. *"A wrong name poisons a century."* | **Vessa-function:** the cold doctrinal antagonist who doesn't need to kill him — needs to *own his meaning*; fights by fragmentation and withheld rites, not force. **Naim-function:** the fractured insider-defector who reveals the temple's *real* inner doctrine, gives a concrete lever, and **will not kneel.** **Brannoc-function:** the blind memory of forbidden rites — dread, not exposition; *prior carriers ended badly.* |
| **The Blood-Keeper Houses** (old-blood / bloodline control) | Genealogists who claim or hunt carriers of the Red; the in-city echo of the hunted-bloodline; believe *some blood remembers before the mind does*; may hold a fragment of who Rook's murdered house was. | He may be *theirs* — or the thing they were sworn to end before another faction weaponizes it. | **Asha-function:** the informed-fear genealogist who recognizes the line before he does, offers the first identity beyond "monster," **marks terms, not obedience,** and holds the caller/kill-check. **Cairn-function:** the socialized mirror / possible executioner — raised inside the city's law, everything Rook is not; tells him the truth without worship; carries the kill-check at the caller rite. |

**The rise-sequence** (ported wholesale as the shape of his climb; the
seven-beat First Political Sequence from `saqarro-and-the-peoples.md` §8):
brokers find the prize first and hide it → the temple seizes him in a
jurisdiction dispute to fix his meaning → the bloodline-houses recognize the
markers and argue against destroying him → the arena-masters demand the rite →
he is thrown in as compromise/punishment/test at once → **the Red wakes** → the
arena-masters claim the result before the temple can explain it away. That last
beat is the leverage flashpoint the whole book turns on.

## 4. THE CAST (threads carried & introduced)

**Carried from Book 1:**

- **Marrow** — the Brother, in the pit beside him from line one (ch28 handoff).
  The band is already two. His arc: from the only kin in the dark to the heart of
  the *unite-the-bled* movement; the one who keeps Rook human while the count
  gets colder; the loyal warning voice when the Red learns administration. His
  body is still his whole voice (high shoulder, curled hand, rocking gait). One
  hard cost late: a wound that should kill him, and the temptation it puts in
  front of Rook to use the Red to save him — the anchor and the corruption in one
  body.
- **Voss-Marr** — the distant nemesis. He is NOT in the city; he is back on
  Saqarro holding the broken Vorth and the wrong of the crown that rotted in him.
  Kept as a deferred, off-page pressure (rumor, a captured Vorth's read, a hauled
  trophy) — *the war born from a Book-1 mercy is still coming.* He surfaces at the
  end as a Book-3 vector: the slavers' next harvest, or the island Rook must go
  back for. **Do not resolve him here.**
- **The Vorth / Mure / Skell harvested across the water** — the island monsters
  are *here,* at the bottom, bled with everyone else. Grist (the spared Skell of
  Book 1) is the bridge: a captive Skell in the sumps reads the new order first,
  as the leavings always do, and is the first sign Rook's crown crosses species
  lines into *unite the bled.* The Mure in the bleeding-houses are the horror the
  doctrine can't look at: the Beast rung of the ladder, caged and tithed — *what
  Rook could become, milked for what it is.*
- **Yara** — the deferred ember. **Not present at the start;** Book 1 left her in
  the hold, freed-or-lost. She is the only head that carries a *clean name* for
  the Eye-and-Thorn. She returns mid-book as a thread — surfacing somewhere in the
  middle tiers (a broker's translator; a freed-but-owing scribe) — and her
  Book-1-seeded knowledge of the sigil is a lever toward the heritage. Romance
  stays an ember, not a plotline; recognition deepens. **Do not consummate; do
  not over-spend.** She is also the open seam to the series' "old-blood Yara" —
  whether she *is* that figure or its precursor is left live.
- **Drevan Sool** — likely dying or dead of Saqarro's fever by the time the
  pit-city has Rook (the dramatic-irony B-plot pays off). But **the trade
  outlives the trader:** his *sale* — "this one does not die; watch" — is the read
  the arena-masters bought. His ghost is the price-tag on Rook's first chapter.
- **The Seer** — left on Saqarro; her sensed-but-unnamed read of the blood is the
  question the city detonates and fails to settle. Echo only.

**Introduced in Book 2** (named when written; functions locked above): the
old patron-predator (Harvek-fn); the pit-singer/broadcaster (renamed
Yara-Khar-fn); the dangerous devotee (Dremm-fn); the broker-realist (Maren-fn);
the doctrinal antagonist (Vessa-fn); the temple defector who won't kneel
(Naim-fn); the blind memory (Brannoc-fn); the genealogist who holds the
kill-switch (Asha-fn); the socialized mirror/executioner (Cairn-fn); the
grief-keeper who weaponizes the empty cages (Mother-Harl-fn); the
living-guarantee child (Senn-fn) whose share, counted into the ledger, is the
one the failing count finally shorts.

## 5. PARTS & CHAPTER BEATS

### PART ONE — THE BLED (ch1–6): the bottom; the Red wakes for real; memory returns.

The Red-Rising opening: absolute bottom, the engine ignites, identity detonates.
This is where Book 1's withheld reveal pays off.

1. **Meat and Meaning** — *(ports the frozen ch11 "meat and meaning" function.)*
   The morning after ch28. The arena-masters' floor; Rook and Marrow kept alive
   because the dying slaver-captain *sold the reading* — "this one does not die,
   watch." Establish the vertical city through the body: the smell of the sumps,
   the tithe-runs, the bleeding-houses, the rim-light he cannot reach. The brokers
   find the prize and move to **hide** him (market shock); the masters want him in
   the throat. He is property again, and counting. The still place holds; the Red
   does not stir — yet.
2. **The Throat** — *(ports the pit-descent, frozen ch8: ritual washing-before-
   killing, the death-name, factions stationed at different distances from the
   pit-mouth as a map of power.)* They put him in the arena to *see what his blood
   does.* The rite is staged so each faction watches from its own tier. He is meant
   to perform monster or die. The crowd looks at him "like a question."
3. **The Red, Awake** — *(ports the transformation/first-kill, frozen ch9 — the
   engine's ignition; "Survival is not innocence.")* The arena floor is centuries
   of spilled blood; his own opens against it. **The Red arrives — not Book 1's
   flicker, but all at once.** Not strength poured into empty places — a door. He
   wins horrifically. The worst image is not the kill but what comes *with* it:
   the first images breaking up through the blood. **Blood = ancestral memory:**
   the murder-night, the smuggled child, the Eye-and-Thorn as a thing his *house*
   wore, not a brand the slavers laid. The reveal Book 1 withheld lands here,
   mid-kill, fractured and partial. He climbs out **wrong.**
4. **Returned** — *(ports the boy-returns-wrong, frozen ch10: the crowd that
   looked "like a question" now looks "like an answer"; the broadcaster breaks the
   death-name and renames him; worship; he vomits — shame is the one thing the Red
   recoils from.)* The pit-singer (broadcaster-fn) *names* the event for the
   writing-less city before the temple can — myth outruns the boy in a single
   breath. The arena-masters claim him; the temple is too late; the brokers'
   prices reset. He refuses the worship and claims his own small name aloud across
   the bled: **"Rook."** Core beat: *decide whether to stand as yourself or be
   worshiped as your wound.*
5. **What the Blood Remembers** — the cost of ch3's reveal. Memory comes back in
   the Red's coin, not clean: gaps, the wrong fear gone, a hunger for the rite. He
   reaches for *who his house was* and the same wall meets him — partial, never
   whole, never confirmed (hold the three theories). The bloodline-houses
   (Asha-fn) recognize the markers and make their move: *useful, or theirs, or the
   thing to end.* First contact with the genealogist who can name a fragment of
   him — and who marks terms, not obedience. Marrow is the anchor that keeps the
   memory from drowning him.
6. **Grist in the Sump** — he finds the island among the bled: a captive Skell
   (Grist-bridge), caged Mure in the bleeding-houses, Vorth hauled across the
   water. *Unite the clans → unite the bled* begins, at the absolute bottom, with
   the discarded who read the order first. End of Part One: he has the Red, a
   partial name, a brother, the first of the bled — and four factions now each
   needing a different thing from his blood.

### PART TWO — THE USE OF HIM (ch7–13): the rise through the faction machine.

The tribe-by-tribe tests of the frozen Parts 4–5, re-cast as faction tests; the
rise runs on playing their uses against each other. Compress the frozen
connective tissue — keep the *function* of each test, not five near-identical
challenges.

7. **The Broker's Ledger** — *(ports the Salt economic test, frozen ch13.)* The
   broker-realist (Maren-fn) arrives with ledgers, not worship — *who eats, who
   guards, who profits from panic.* The first lesson that **real power here is the
   counting,** not the pit. He cuts his first deal: protection of the bled in
   exchange for the routes the masters can't run without him. He is bad at it; the
   still place is good at it.
8. **The Meaning-Fight** — *(ports the interpretive-authority seizure /
   jurisdiction dispute.)* The temple (Vessa-fn) moves to **own his meaning** —
   redefine him as *breach, not blessing,* a pollution of the tithe. The
   doctrinal antagonist who fights calmly, by fragmentation and withheld rites.
   Rook can't out-argue doctrine; he changes the venue (see ch10). First taste of
   *the broadcaster beats the truth* working *against* him.
9. **The Devotee** — *(ports the Dremm function and the first-restraint seed,
   frozen ch12: handed a kill everyone expects, he spares instead; "not killing
   could make the crowd hold its breath too"; "Mercy is not clean — sometimes it
   is only violence made to wait.")* The dangerous devotee (Dremm-fn) loves the
   monster first; Rook's restraint reads to him as weakness — the permanent
   internal fracture of leading a consumption faction with non-consumption law. A
   sparing that creates a more dangerous follower than a killing would have.
10. **The Second Throat** — *(ports the frozen ch18 reversal — the best political-
    judo scene; the no-win second-pit trap countered by changing the rules.)* The
    temple proposes a *second* arena rite: perform monster or *prove absence* — a
    trap with no exit. He counters by changing the rules — a public reckoning at
    the tithe-stones, witnessed by all four factions, no song, no wound, no
    hunger. He sits it out refusing to let the Red speak, answering only *"I am
    Rook."* Neither side wins. The leash mechanic seeded: power that he holds by
    *not* using the thing they want to see.
11. **The Caller's Terms** — *(ports the crown-with-terms / "crown that bites,"
    frozen ch20 — the centerpiece; keep intact.)* He will not take leadership of
    the bled until he can set **terms,** and the terms ARE the crown:
    - *The Red is not king. The blood-crowned answers to law before appetite.*
    - *He cannot call rite, raid, or sacrifice alone.*
    - *If he "goes red" and will not answer to his own name, an outside hand may
      call him back.*
    - *If he does not return, that hand must bind or kill him before following the
      monster.*
    The **caller is deliberately a rival faction** — the bloodline-houses
    (Asha-fn), not his own base — so the leash can't be captured by his power.
    The crown is not gold but a brow-band of bled-stone / carved shell-and-tooth,
    each plate cut with an old tithe-law. It is *leash and weapon* — it bites the
    wearer first. **Recall ritual:** Asha-fn keeps a token of his blood/hair; a
    wrapped relic struck against the tithe-stone and his name spoken three times
    pulls him back; the socialized mirror (Cairn-fn) carries it unwrapped as a
    standing threat.
12. **The Inner Doctrine** — *(ports the Naim defector, frozen ch30, radically
    simplified — drop the genealogy weeds, keep the lever.)* A low priest of the
    temple defects with *information, not loyalty:* the **real** tithe-doctrine the
    courts have been simplifying — what the bleeding is *actually* for, what the
    "Cold kept at bay" actually costs. He gives Rook a concrete lever (a sign that
    can "crack a rite") and **will not kneel.** His public silences become a wedge:
    *even the priestling won't back the crown.* The Red **learns from
    unritualized vulnerability** — a new kind of pit.
13. **The Blind Memory** — *(ports Brannoc — dread, not exposition.)* The blind
    keeper of forbidden rites gives Rook not answers but **prior carriers, and how
    they ended.** Implies the factions didn't create the blood-force — they only
    built customs around where it leaks. First on-page seeding that the carrier's
    road has an end, and the end is cold. End of Part Two: he is crowned-with-
    terms over the bled and the working tiers, leashed by a rival, useful to all
    four, owned by none — and the Red is more articulate every time he holds it.

### PART THREE — THE COUNT (ch14–20): rule-as-counting; the Red learns administration; the bled rise.

The freshest layer (frozen Part 6), compressed hard per the mining brief: **two**
representative ledger scenes, not five — a counting *win,* a counting *strain,* a
counting *failure that kills someone* — on a rising ladder, not a flat loop.

14. **The Count Opened** — *(ports "open the store, count aloud," frozen ch29:
    "Count what is. Name what is not. Aloud.")* Rook's reform engine, imported from
    Book 1's *consume-one → living blood-debt:* he makes the tithe a **law** before
    his followers make it an **appetite.** The bleeding-books are opened and counted
    aloud in public — who is bled, who is owed, who eats. The broker-realist takes
    the counting; the grief-keeper (Mother-Harl-fn) puts the *families of the bled*
    into the accounting so the men who love the rite "stand still while women name
    what the tithe has already taken." A counting **win.**
15. **Useful Sacrifice** — *(ports the frozen ch31 "useful sacrifice" — convert one
    death into living blood-debt.)* Under the first shortfall, the arena-masters'
    sub-faction reopens the old case: *the Cold is at the rim — bleed someone, the
    old price.* Rook converts the execution demand into **living blood-debt** —
    sworn-labor, reassigned shares, a debt-bowl that travels instead of a corpse.
    A win — but the Red **files the move away.** First sign it has stopped wanting
    blood and started wanting the *clean administrative answer.* **This is the
    danger signal,** not the victory.
16. **Unite the Bled** — the underclass uprising crests. The bled, the island-
    monsters, the wrong-bloods, the freed-but-owing — the discarded who read the
    order first — turn to the king who counts what the strong waste. *King of the
    wild things → king of all the owned.* The middle tiers are his. The brokers
    bend (it's good for routes); the masters seethe (the mob obeys appetite, and
    appetite now has a name that isn't theirs); the temple calls it heresy; the
    houses watch their carrier become a *power.* The high-water mark — paid for in
    Part Four.
17. **The Strain** — *(ports the frozen fracture, ch33–34, collapsed: every promise
    becomes a mouth he can't feed.)* The tithe yields less; the brokers raise the
    price; the count strains. The reach exceeds the administrative capacity — the
    boy-king scaling problem: every voice sworn under his name "creates more places
    for other hands to reach before the knot can hold." A counting **strain.**
18. **What the Red Learned** — *(ports the frozen ch33 horror-turn: "the name can
    starve its own promises and still grow stronger… choose who the Cold takes
    first without a pit or a blood snow.")* The Red, fully articulate now, offers
    the **administrative solution:** let the weak die *usefully,* by ledger, no
    rite required. The horror shifts fully from cannibal-hunger to **the cold logic
    of triage.** Training the thing to be restrained taught it to wear the crown.
    Marrow is the anchor that names what the count is becoming — read off his body,
    not in thesis. The first time Rook cannot tell whether a ruling was his or the
    Red's.
19. **The Shorted Share** — *(ports the frozen ch36 dead-child fracture — the
    strongest material in the source; keep it nearly verbatim, re-skinned.)* The
    counting **failure that kills someone.** A child counted into the share — the
    living-guarantee (Senn-fn), a walking bond between factions — dies waiting for
    the share the strained count finally shorts. The order built to protect the
    bled takes from the bled. The cost lands publicly, with witnesses. *Every
    promise the count makes becomes a mouth it can't feed.*
20. **Vessa's Clean Knife** — *(ports the best antagonist move in the frozen book,
    ch28: the temple stops trying to leash him and sends *medicine and healers* to
    the bled — making his allies ashamed to stand near him; "a woman with clean
    hands making allies ashamed.")* The temple weaponizes the shorted share: it
    grieves *for* the bled, aims the accusation at *Rook's* count, closes the
    mourning-rites to whoever stands with him. Politics by guilt and withheld rites,
    not force. The Red doesn't stir — which frightens Rook more than if it had.
    End of Part Three: he holds the under-city, and it is eating him from the
    middle out.

### PART FOUR — THE WRAPPED CALL (ch21–25): the leash tested for real; the cost lands.

The crown-that-bites mechanic fires. The Red surges; the kill-switch is used.

21. **Blood on the Flats** — *(ports "Blood Snow," frozen ch19 — keep the battle
    structure and the half-transformation signature; re-skin the battlefield to a
    tidal flat where blood shows.)* The first real battle of the war for the
    under-city: a faction coalition moves to break him. A feint and a flanking
    march; the plot turns on the devotee (Dremm-fn) **breaking formation for
    revenge** and getting the bled killed — *followers ask permission after
    spending your name.* Rook lets the Red rise **only halfway** — worse, because he
    can *see* what he chooses. The Red wins the fight. It does not win the after.
22. **Half-Made** — *(ports the Dremm-mutilation reversal, frozen ch20→23: sent to
    carry his own kill to the enemy line unarmed, he comes back maimed and becomes a
    martyr-figure; "his mutilation did what mercy had not — it gave him shape.")*
    Mercy creates a more dangerous opponent than cruelty would have: the devotee,
    maimed and martyred, becomes a faction badge the young of the bled copy.
    Rook's *restraint* manufactures his most dangerous internal enemy.
23. **The Live Call** — *(ports the frozen ch35 "wrapped call," kept as the ONE
    recall scene — the source ran it three times; we run it once.)* The Red surges
    past the half — full takeover threatened in front of the whole under-city. The
    caller-faction (Asha-fn) must use the crown term: the wrapped relic struck
    against the tithe-stone, his name spoken three times, the socialized mirror
    (Cairn-fn) standing ready to **bind or kill** if he does not come. He comes
    back — barely — and the Red **weaponizes the call,** turns the discipline into
    a bargain, uses his own tools against him. The leash holds; it costs.
24. **The Cost** — the ledger of the live call comes due (the magic's costing
    rule). Memory gaps; a relationship damaged; followers who now openly prefer the
    monster; the question of whether the choices in the battle were his. The
    socialized mirror (Cairn-fn) tells him the truth without worship: *you are
    becoming the thing the caller was made to stop.* The hardest beat: **Marrow
    wounded** — a hurt that should kill him, and the Red whispering that it could
    save him, for a price down the ladder. The anchor and the corruption in one
    body. Rook chooses the count over the Red — and may lose Marrow to it, or keep
    him at a cost. *(Keep it hard; do not let the Red be the clean rescue.)*
25. **The Ember and the Sigil** — Yara returns to the thread proper. The one head
    that carries a clean name for the Eye-and-Thorn meets the carrier who wears it,
    now that the Red has given him fragments of his own house. Two halves of one
    knowledge, finally in one room — and still not enough to *solve* the Red (hold
    the three theories). Recognition, not romance; an ember, banked. She is also
    the first to say aloud what the blind memory implied: the carrier's road has an
    end. End of Part Four: he holds the under-city, leashed and costing, the Red
    learning to wear him, the bled his — and the thing the tithe was built to hold
    begins to come.

### PART FIVE — THE COLD AT THE WALLS (ch26–31): the tithe fails; the Cold comes; the Book-3 cliffhanger.

The over-engine fires: the failing tithe + the approaching Cold collide in the
city, with the one carrier whose blood can end them at the bottom of it. Escalate
toward Book 3.

26. **The Tithe Fails** — the doctrine stops being academic. The bleedings yield
    less; the rim goes cold where it never did; the first **husks** appear — neat,
    bloodless, no wound, no rot (the prologue's horror, now at the gates). The
    temple's load-bearing lie cracks in public: *the tithe is failing, and the Cold
    is coming anyway.* The factions fracture over the answer — *more tithe* (bleed
    more), *the living carrier* (own Rook, the ultimate ward), or *end the line*
    (the houses' old fear). All three roads run through his blood.
27. **More Tithe** — the arena-masters and the temple converge on the oldest
    answer: bleed the bled to the walls. The "useful sacrifice under scarcity"
    pressure, maxed: *the Cold is at the rim — bleed someone.* Rook, who spent the
    whole book turning *consume-one* into living law, must now hold the line
    against the city's instinct to feed itself to its own fear — while the Red
    offers, coldly, to choose who goes first.
28. **Blood Burns the Cold** — *(the unifier mythology pays off — held UNSOLVED.)*
    The Cold breach a tier. In the breach, Rook's blood does the thing the whole
    economy was built on a rumor of: **Red-blood is the one thing the Bloodless
    cannot survive** — fire and poison to them, the single force that ends them.
    The doctrine was *true* — and that truth makes him the city's salvation and its
    beacon at once: a living carrier either wards the Cold or **draws it down on
    everyone near him.** Same blood, two readings, irreconcilable (the
    slaver-captain's greed-and-fear, now at city scale). He is the jackpot and the
    target, confirmed — and still nobody, including Rook, knows *what* the Red is.
29. **The Buried Irony** — *(seed for Book 3; never stated flat.)* The blind memory
    and the defector converge on the thing none of them will say: *leaning on the
    blood to keep the Cold out is how you become the Cold.* The tithe that holds the
    husks at bay is the road to becoming a husk; the city is the corruption ladder
    writ large; Rook, burning the Cold with his own blood low after low, is walking
    his own road to the husk faster than anyone. The Red, fully administrative now,
    finds this *efficient.* Hold the deep-link unconfirmed; let the reader assemble
    it.
30. **The Choice at the Walls** — Rook makes the season's defining choice: not what
    the Red wants (feed the city to the Cold and rule the ash), not what the boy
    wants (run, take Marrow and the bled and go). He spends himself — his blood, the
    count, the bled united — to hold the walls *this once,* knowing what each use of
    the Red costs him down the ladder. The under-city holds. Barely. Through the
    combination of the Red (the fight) and the count and the kept (the peace) —
    never the Red alone. He is the beast now, with the boy still alive inside,
    refusing to die. The central tension does not resolve; it escalates into
    Book 3.
31. **The Cliffhanger** — three vectors fire at once, handing Book 3:
    - **The Cold.** They withdraw from the breach — not beaten, *learning.* The
      husks at the rim multiply. They have found the carrier they have hunted the
      line for; the tithe-economy that hid him is failing; *they will come for the
      city to get to him,* and the city now knows it. The It-Follows engine, at
      scale.
    - **The world.** A signal from across the water — a fleet, an embassy, a
      rumor: the wider world has heard of *the blood that woke in the pit-city* and
      *the pit that answers with laughter.* The island that was the whole world is
      now a piece on a larger board.
    - **The island.** **Voss-Marr** surfaces — the slavers' next harvest run, or a
      hauled Vorth trophy, or a runner: the broken Vorth under the rival who won't
      bow are still out there, and Saqarro is the ground Rook may have to go back
      for. The war born from a Book-1 mercy is still coming.
    Final image (rhyming ch28's close): Rook at the rim of the city he climbed,
    the count going, Marrow at his shoulder, the bled behind him, the Red low and
    waiting, the husks below the horizon. *Still counting. And now the whole world
    is counting back.* → Book 3 ("The Great Man" — the ice/Cold and the world
    arrive; consolidation against the Bloodless and the failing tithe).

## 6. MYTHOLOGY PAYOFFS (what Book 2 detonates — and what it keeps buried)

**Detonates (the reveals Book 1 withheld):**

- **The Red wakes for real** (ch3) — the awakened, active power, not Book 1's
  omen. Ignited by the arena's centuries of spilled blood (spilled blood is the
  throttle).
- **Ancestral memory returns** (ch3, 5, 25) — the murder-night, the smuggled
  child, the Eye-and-Thorn as his *house's* mark. Power and memory are the same
  system; the memory comes back in the Red's coin, partial and costly.
- **Blood burns the Cold** (ch28) — the unifier switch confirmed on the page: his
  blood is the one thing that ends the Bloodless. This makes him salvation and
  beacon at once and explains, retroactively, every faction's different nightmare.
- **The tithe-doctrine was true** (ch26–28) — the "Cold kept at bay" was not pure
  fiction; the bleeding really did hold something off. Which makes the failing
  tithe a real apocalypse, not a political one.

**Kept UNSOLVED (the Bloodborne rule — three theories stand, none confirmed):**

- *What the Red is* — fed god / bloodline taint / stolen slain-god's blood. The
  city, the houses, the defector, the blind memory, Yara, and Rook all reach for
  one and none reaches the middle. Book 2 gives him *fragments of his house,* never
  the answer.
- *Who killed his family, and who his house was* — surfaced as fragments, left
  contested. The Cold, men greedy for the blood, betrayers selling the house — all
  three still live.
- *The deep-link* (the Cold are the far end of the carrier's own ladder; leaning
  on the blood to fight the Cold is how you become the Cold) — seeded as dread
  (ch29), never stated flat.

**The cost ledger (every major Red use alters the story):** ch3 (memory comes
back broken, the wrong fear gone); ch9/22 (restraint manufactures worse
enemies); ch18 (the Red learns administration — the danger signal); ch23–24 (the
live call's costs: gaps, damaged bonds, followers who prefer the monster, the
unanswerable *was-it-mine*); ch24 (Marrow wounded — the anchor against the
corruption, in one choice); ch28–30 (every use that burns the Cold is a step
toward becoming it).

## 7. CAST ARCS (one line each)

- **Rook:** bled at the bottom → the Red awake and his house half-remembered →
  crowned-with-terms over the bled → ruler-by-counting → the carrier who burns the
  Cold and walks his own road to the husk doing it. Boy → Beast, advancing; the
  boy not dead.
- **Marrow:** the only kin in the dark → the heart of unite-the-bled → the anchor
  who names what the count is becoming → wounded, the corruption's temptation made
  flesh; kept or lost at a cost, not by the Red's clean rescue.
- **The Red:** omen → awake → articulate → **administrative.** Its arc is the
  corruption ladder; its growing fluency is the horror, not the help.
- **Yara:** deferred ember → returns mid-book → the clean name for the sigil meets
  the carrier who wears it → recognition banked, the carrier's-road truth first
  spoken. Romance unspent; the old-blood-Yara seam left open.
- **Voss-Marr:** off-page pressure (rumor, trophy, the broken island) → surfaces at
  the cliffhanger as a Book-3 vector. Unresolved by design.
- **The four factions:** arena-masters (claim him, then can't hold the mob);
  brokers (price him, then need him); temple (own his meaning, then send the clean
  knife, then crack when the doctrine proves true); houses (recognize him, leash
  him, hold the kill-switch, watch their carrier become a power). The machine never
  stabilizes; he survives in the gap.
- **The bled:** the harvested island-monsters and wrong-bloods (Grist-bridge, caged
  Mure, hauled Vorth) → the underclass that turns to the king who counts what the
  strong waste → the army-nucleus and the conscience of Book 3.

## 8. BOOK 2 → BOOK 3 HANDOFF

Book 3 ("The Great Man") inherits, from the ch31 cliffhanger:

- **The Cold, learning and coming** — the tithe failing, the husks multiplying,
  the hunters who have finally found their carrier. The series' external apocalypse
  moves from rumor (the prologue) to siege.
- **The carrier's road to the husk** — Rook burning the Cold with his own blood is
  the fastest way down the ladder; Book 3's internal engine is whether the count
  and the kept can hold the boy in the beast while the world demands he be the
  weapon.
- **The world arriving** — the wider blood-economy and its powers, heard of him,
  coming as ally/weapon/threat. The board widens past the island and the city.
- **The island unfinished** — Voss-Marr and the broken Vorth; the Seer's unsettled
  read; Saqarro as ground Rook may have to retake. The Book-1 mercy's war,
  deferred, still due.
- **Yara, the ember and the key** — the deferred relationship and the
  half-knowledge of the heritage, both live into Book 3.
- **The Red, unsolved** — three theories still standing, now with the stakes of a
  confirmed apocalypse riding on which is true.
