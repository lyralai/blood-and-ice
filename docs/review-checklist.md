# Review Checklist

Use this checklist before expanding manuscript or merging major creative changes.

## IP Cleanliness

- No old character names.
- No old place names.
- No old house/family names.
- No old franchise-specific objects, titles, creatures, religions, or political structures.
- No direct references to the old source franchise in public-facing project docs.
- No direct instruction to imitate a named living author's style.

## Story Foundation

- Rook has a clear opening condition.
- The Blackjaw Isles feel culturally specific.
- Each of the four tribes has a material motive.
- Khar Vane cannibal practice has rules and costs.
- The White Pit is a political compromise, not random cruelty.
- The Red has limits.
- Every magic use costs something.

## Book One

- The first book can stand without rushing to mainland conquest.
- The act structure escalates from survival to politics to myth to leadership.
- The ending creates a new problem rather than pretending the island is solved.

## Voice

- Rook sounds physical, suspicious, and age-appropriate at the start.
- First-person intensity is balanced with clarity.
- Violence changes story state.
- Dread enters through body and witness before lore.

## Next Draft Gate

Before drafting beyond the opening:

- choose final names for the four tribes
- decide exact geography of the White Pit
- decide whether Rook has a nonhuman companion
- create `docs/continuity/cost-ledger.md`
- draft chapters in act packets, not endless autonomous loops
