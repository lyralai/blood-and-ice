# Pitch

## Working Title

**Blood Under Ice**

## Logline

A feral northern boy is sacrificed by island tribes and survives because an ancient blood-force wakes inside him; when the most feared cannibal tribe crowns him as its omen, he must learn whether leadership is different from possession before the island turns him into a god-shaped weapon.

## The Appeal

This is a dark coming-of-power story with three engines running at once:

- **Survival**: the boy begins nearly wordless, cold, hungry, and animal-clever.
- **Politics**: four tribes compete over food, ritual authority, bloodline legitimacy, and fear.
- **Cosmic horror**: the power that saves him is older than human loyalty and does not care whether he remains himself.

The early story should feel physical and immediate: black rock, seal fat, old blood, bone tools, hunger, cold sea, animal tracks, ritual smoke. The larger story should scale from boy-survival to tribal leadership to religious war to mainland consequence.

## What Makes It Work

Rook does not become important because he is chosen by a clean prophecy. He becomes important because several groups misread the same event in ways that serve them:

- Khar Vane wants proof that their blood rites are still favored.
- The Hollow Antler priesthood wants to control the event and name it.
- The Salt Veyr traders want to survive the political shock.
- The Fen-Kin want the old northern bloodline either claimed or killed.
- Rook wants food, warmth, and control over the thing that answers when he bleeds.

The result is unstable. He gets followers before he understands power. The tribe most willing to follow him is also the tribe most likely to make him monstrous.

## Tone

First-person, intimate, brutal, and controlled. The prose should move fast in action, slow down for dread, and treat beauty as something dangerous because beauty is what people use to justify sacrifice.

The story should never wink at genre tropes. Hunger matters. Wounds matter. Political choices have logistics. Magic has cost. Every victory should create a new debt.

## Comparable Creative Territory

Use these as broad shelves, not imitation targets:

- visceral first-person ascent fantasy
- tribal political epic
- mythic dark coming-of-age
- possession horror
- northern survival fiction
- cosmic blood religion

## Target Reader Promise

The reader should come for the feral-boy-to-tribal-king escalation and stay because the power that makes him useful may also erase the very person worth following.
