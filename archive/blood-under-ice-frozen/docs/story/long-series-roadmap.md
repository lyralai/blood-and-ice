# Long Series Roadmap

The 500k-word ambition is plausible only if the project is structured as a sequence of escalating problems rather than one endless conquest. Each book needs a different central question.

## Series Spine

Rook's power grows in three directions:

1. **Body**: survival, healing, violence, possession.
2. **Tribe**: followers, law, food, ritual, legitimacy.
3. **World**: mainland politics, old bloodlines, religious panic, imperial consequence.

The danger is that the Red grows along the same paths. It learns body first, then tribe, then world.

## Book One: Blood Under Ice

**Question:** Can Rook survive being made sacred by the people most likely to feed his monster?

Scope:

- Blackjaw Isles
- four tribes
- White Pit
- Khar Vane following
- first leadership crisis (blood-crown under law, cradle-store and road systems, small-tribe integration, internal faction pressure, and the Red learning counting/administrative hunger while the boy holds the crown term)

Target length: 120k-160k words.

Current state note (Packet 5 complete; Packet 6 specs expanded + some chapters drafted): The leadership crisis has moved from initial following to structural systems (contracts, public counting, road-sworn, small loops). The Guest is learning from the tools used to contain it; winter scarcity, Dremm-faction "useful" offers, and small-loop extension are the pressure test. Mid-Book One crisis now clearer: whether the political order can survive winter without the monster becoming the practical government that names the due and collects it with teeth the counting cannot match. Boy-king problem escalated by every new voice under the name creating more places for other hands to reach before the knot can hold.

## Book Two: The Hunger Crown

**Question:** Can a boy with a war tribe build law before his followers turn appetite into government?

Scope:

- island consolidation
- Salt Veyr logistics
- Hollow Antler counter-religion
- Fen-Kin bloodline revelations
- first organized anti-Rook coalition
- Rook's first deliberate reforms of Khar Vane practice

Target length: 120k-150k words.

## Book Three: The Cold Mainland

**Question:** What happens when an island-made omen enters a mainland that needs symbols more than truth?

Scope:

- first mainland landing
- northern houses/clans without borrowed IP structure
- old-blood recognition
- mainland church or crown response
- Rook used as claimant, weapon, and scandal
- The Red learns mass politics

Target length: 150k-180k words.

## Book Four: The Red Law

**Question:** Can Rook rule without building a society that rewards possession?

Scope:

- war leadership
- reforms and betrayals
- followers split between Rook-the-boy and Rook-the-monster
- old blood versus blood-god theology
- Cairn/Yara/Dremm consequences
- major defeat caused by overuse of the Red

Target length: 140k-180k words.

## Book Five: The Guest Below

**Question:** If the Red can become a ruler, what must Rook give up to remain a person?

Scope:

- cosmic horror revelations
- prior vessels
- false victory over the blood-force
- final political settlement
- cost of separating leadership from hunger

Target length: 120k-160k words.

## Production Strategy

Do not generate chronologically forever without review. Use staged expansion:

1. Lock bible and outline.
2. Draft book one part one.
3. Review voice, names, politics, and magic cost.
4. Draft by act, not by endless loop.
5. After each act, update continuity docs.
6. Keep a running `docs/continuity/` folder once manuscript drafting begins.

## Continuity Rules

- Every new power use must update the cost ledger.
- Every public miracle must update political reaction.
- Every tribe needs food logic in long arcs.
- Rook's voice may mature but should not become generic court narration.
- The Red should learn from repeated use.
- No victory without a social consequence.

## Scale Notes

A 500k-word project needs repetition control. The late raw source failed because it repeated one symbolic register until plot vanished. This roadmap avoids that by assigning different engines to different books:

- Book one: survival and tribal myth.
- Book two: law and island statecraft.
- Book three: culture shock and mainland politics.
- Book four: rule and rebellion.
- Book five: metaphysical cost and final identity.

The monster can recur. The story cannot become only the monster.
