# Book One Production Plan

## Target

Book One should be drafted in controlled packets, not as an endless generation stream.

The near-term target is a reviewable 25k-35k word opening package that proves:

- Rook's voice can carry a long first-person novel.
- The four tribes create plot without relying on outside lore.
- The White Pit is inevitable by politics, not random cruelty.
- The Red is useful, frightening, and costly.

The long target is a full Book One of roughly 140k-180k words. A 500k-word series becomes plausible only if Book One has strong continuity systems before scaling.

## Draft Packets

### Packet 1: Wild Boy And Salt Law

**Status:** rough draft complete.

Chapters:

1. Black Shore
2. Hunger Law
3. First Blood In The Trees
4. Salt Knots

Does Rook feel like a feral fourteen-year-old who is smart enough to learn politics, or does he sound too polished?

25. Cradle Tenth

## Chapter Standard

Each chapter should do at least three jobs:

- move the plot
- alter one relationship
- clarify one tribal rule
- deepen the Red cost
- change Rook's public meaning
- reveal a new pressure on food, law, or ritual

If a chapter only repeats that Rook is dangerous, cut or rebuild it.
