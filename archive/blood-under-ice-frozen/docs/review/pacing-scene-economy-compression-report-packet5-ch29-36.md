However, pacing suffers from **post-beat reflection bloat** — especially night walks and chapter closers that restate the political idea after it has already landed via action, physical tells, and witness reactions.

**Primary problems flagged:**
1. **Ch31 duplicate artifact** (critical hygiene per consolidated punch list): Near-identical "Dremm's face had gone flat" + "three cuts and work a sacrifice" exchange appears in a way that repeats the faction reaction after the transformation has already been shown.

4. **Scenes doing only one job**: Several night walks function purely as summary reflection after the dramatic action/cost has already been paid in public (e.g. ch32 end, ch36 end). Last 1/3 of scene often ends on abstract summary instead of concrete action or unresolved cost.
5. **Word economy on reflections**: Target 30-60% cut on these sections. Political ideas (Guest learning crown tools, boy-king strain from small loops, center thinning) are valuable but must be shown more through body/witness and carried forward rather than restated.

**Overall impact:** These slow momentum in a packet that is otherwise high-density on systems strain. Compressing will make the political ideas land harder because they will feel earned rather than explained.

## Pacing Rules for Packet 6+ (One-Pager)

**Core Principle:** Every scene must earn its length. The last third of any scene (especially after a public rite, transformation, or claim) must end on **action, concrete cost, or unresolved physical/witness pressure** — never on summary reflection that restates the political idea the action just delivered.

**What must happen in the last 1/3 of a scene:**
- A concrete next action Rook takes (walks to racks, sends someone, grips the spear, tests a knot, says the name out loud to someone).
- A visible physical cost that lingers (hand still shaking when he lowers it, iron taste that does not fade, red at edges that takes longer to clear, palm pressed to bite, shoulders pulling in).
- A witness tell that carries meaning without explanation (Keva's fingers closing on the loop, girl pressing basket to hip and sideways check, Runa's hand moving small in warning, Naim's knuckles white on cloth, Yara's single low note, Maren's mouth going thin as she marks the board).
- An image that travels (debt bowl sitting visible on stone, small loop heavier than the cord, crude antler cut in mud and spat on, root bundle left on lowest rack, cut cord returned with black sand loop).

**How to end on action/cost instead of summary:**
- Bad: "...The Red had taken a new shape inside the law. That was the useful part. That was also the part that frightened me most." (After the rite has already shown it.)
- Good: "The debt bowl sat on the stone where anyone could see the mark. The three were already on the lower pass with tools and no weapons. My hand still tasted iron."
- Bad: Long night walk explaining "the island was larger... more voices made the center thinner... the flats would remember who came first..."
- Good: Stop at the concrete images from the scene (Keva fingers, girl basket, antler cut) + physical (hand shook) + one forward pressure sentence ("It would remember the shape when the next claim came").

**Guest surge + reflection hygiene:**
- After a major beat (road-sworn transformation, small loop knot, due payment), show the surge once with specific images ("debt bowl poured across threshold", "tide flats as a thing a hand could close", "name deciding who the frost took").
- Then anchor (self-name spoken under breath, counting witnesses aloud, wrapped spear grip, "small loop" spoken).
- End the surge with one physical tell + what Rook does next. Do not add a second paragraph explaining what the Guest "learned" or "filed like a man testing a new knife" unless it is a new escalation.
- Never repeat the "island larger / more voices = thinner center / hand still shook / Guest had heard the name" block in consecutive chapters. Vary the image or move the political clarity into dialogue (Asha, Harvek, Maren) or a later strategic scene.

**Scene economy test before closing a chapter:**
1. Has the key beat (plot move + relationship shift + Guest cost + public meaning) already landed via action and witness?
2. If yes, the last 1/3 can be 3-6 sentences max focused on one lingering image + physical cost + one next action.
3. If the political idea still needs to be felt, show it through a character reacting to the consequence (Maren marking the new due line, Naim choosing silence a second time, Half-Ear faction walking away quiet with the bowl) rather than Rook summarizing it at night.

**Voice reminder (from 21-28 review notes):** Anchor in body, exits counted, hands, what the eyes see next, iron taste, red edges, spear grip. Prefer "I saw the mothers and the empty wood and the way Harvek would not look at them" over "I heard the whole island then... No tribe was a beast alone."

**Packet 6 application:** In ch33-36 reckonings and due escalations, every time a new loop/claim is added or paid, end the scene on the concrete payment or the hand that will carry it (road-sworn walking the due, faction carrying the bowl, Naim's white cloth), not on another "the center was thinner now" paragraph. Let the repetition of concrete costs (thinner shares, coughing children, cut cord, dead child under reed) do the work of showing the strain.
