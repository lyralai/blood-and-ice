# Chapter 7 Draft: Khar Vane Demand

The horn did not sound again.

It did not need to.

After the first note, every person in the antler clearing began arranging fear into custom. Vessa's acolytes took positions by the standing stone. Asha's Fen-Kin spread toward the lower path, reeds and spearpoints becoming one line. Brannoc sat on a root and put the hollow antler across his knees like a child he meant to protect.

No one looked at me.

That was new.

For a little while I was not the most dangerous thing in the clearing. I hated how much relief that gave me.

Cairn kept his knife angled toward my ribs anyway. Sensible boy.

Fog pulled apart at the upper path.

Khar Vane came through without masks.

There were only six of them. They made the clearing feel smaller than twenty would have. Their hair was braided with bone rings. Their cloaks were dark hide patched with pale leather that I did not want to name. Each carried a weapon made for close work: hooked axes, short spears, thick knives. Not pretty weapons. Hungry ones.

The man in front was old in the way cliffs are old. Not bent. Weathered. His mouth had been cut at both corners and healed badly, making his lips look wider when he was not smiling. When he did smile, the scars made him monstrous.

Harvek Bone-Mouth.

I did not know the name, but I knew chief.

Chief is not the largest person. Chief is the one whose silence other people carry.

Beside him walked a woman with a drum at her hip and a strip of red cloth over her eyes. She moved as if she could see better than the rest of us. Bone chimes hung down her sleeves.

Yara Khar.

She stopped when she saw me.

Not looked. Saw.

Her covered face turned toward my wound, my wrist cord, my stolen blood, the place under my ribs where the heat had gone quiet.

Then she began to hum.

The Red woke under my skin like a dog hearing meat fall.

No, I thought.

It did not answer.

Harvek spoke to Vessa first. He did not bow. He did not shout. He gave her exactly the amount of respect a man gives a door he may need to break later.

Vessa answered in ritual speech.

Harvek listened until she was done. That made him more frightening. Men who interrupt are often afraid the words will work if they finish.

He pointed at me.

Then at the standing stone.

Then at the white ground beyond the trees.

Asha stepped forward. Harvek's eyes moved to her and softened by less than a knife's width.

Old enemy, I thought. Old respect. Same thing, sometimes.

They argued.

I caught almost nothing. Names. Blood. Law. Pit. Debt. Return. Nothing.

That last word I understood because Harvek said it while looking at me.

Nothing.

He smiled after.

The kind of smile that shows you the trap after your foot is in it.

Tovek was not there to translate. Maren was not there to count. Senn was not there to glare. I had only the shapes of power and the knife no longer in my hand.

So I watched who wanted what.

Vessa wanted me named before I could become someone else's proof.

Asha wanted me delayed, studied, maybe killed cleanly if I woke wrong.

Harvek wanted the argument to become a place only Khar Vane knew how to stand.

Yara wanted a song.

Brannoc wanted something older than winning.

And me?

I wanted my knife.

Harvek stepped toward me.

Cairn moved to block him. Brave. Stupid. Harvek looked at the boy and laughed once. Not mocking. Almost pleased.

Asha said Cairn's name.

Cairn lowered the knife by one finger's width.

Harvek crouched in front of me until our eyes were level. Up close he smelled of smoke, iron, and dried meat. His scars pulled when he spoke.

He tapped his chest. "Harvek."

I said nothing.

He tapped my wound.

Pain flashed. The heat surged. I bit the inside of my cheek until my own blood filled my mouth.

Harvek watched me hold still.

His smile faded.

Good.

He pointed toward the high white ground. "Pit."

Then he pressed one palm flat to the dirt, lifted it, and held it out empty.

Test.

Maybe.

If nothing, the ground keeps you.

If something, you return.

I understood enough.

I spat blood at his boots.

The Khar Vane behind him went very still.

Harvek looked down. A red spot darkened the pale leather at his toe. He could kill me for that. Everyone knew it. That was why the clearing held its breath.

He laughed.

Not loudly.

Like he had just found a sharp edge on a tool he had expected to be dull.

Yara's humming changed.

Vessa said a word sharp enough to cut rope.

Harvek stood and answered with three words.

This time even I knew the shape. Public. Simple. Final.

Asha cursed under her breath.

Cairn's knife came up again.

Brannoc lifted his hollow antler and spoke into it. The wind carried his voice through the carved holes until it seemed to come from the trees themselves.

The acolytes answered.

The Fen-Kin answered.

Khar Vane answered last, and their answer was not louder, but it landed deepest.

The White Pit.

Law had found the word it wanted.

Vessa came to me and tied a strip of white bark around my upper arm. Asha tied a reed under it. Harvek took a bone ring from his own braid and pressed it into the knot. Three claims in one binding.

Salt Veyr's cord still hung at my wrist under all of it.

Everyone owned a piece of my death.

That was how I learned the seventh law of the island.

When powerful people cannot agree who may kill you, they call the compromise justice.
