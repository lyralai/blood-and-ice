# Chapter 18 Draft: The Second Pit

Vessa came herself after the feast dispute.

She arrived in white bark and antler shadow with twelve acolytes, three old witnesses, and no warriors. That was how I knew she meant war. Warriors make a threat simple. Old witnesses make it last.

Khar Vane gathered before she reached the central fire. Harvek stood at his stone. Solek stood beside the law racks. Dremm stood apart with a load harness still cutting red lines into his shoulders from the marsh road. He looked thinner already. Anger had eaten what work had not.

Maren arrived with Salt Veyr guards because she could smell expensive trouble from the coast.

Asha came too.

Not Cairn. Asha.

The Fen-Kin opened around her like reeds around water. She wore no crown, no charm I could see, only a dark cloak beaded with tiny shells that clicked softly when she walked. Her eyes went first to me, then to Vessa, then to the Khar Vane feast racks.

"Too many mouths," Maren muttered.

Tovek, standing near me, whispered, "When all tribes witness, somebody means to change law."

Vessa stopped outside the fire ring.

She did not look at Harvek first.

She looked at me.

"Rook," she said, using my name like a tool she had cleaned.

I felt the camp react. Names had weight here. For Vessa to use mine was not kindness. It was a hook dressed as respect.

"You were given to the White Pit under mixed claim," she said. "Salt debt. Fen blood. Khar demand. Antler witness. The rite returned no clean answer."

Yara started to translate, but Vessa continued in trade-tongue, each word precise enough to bruise.

"The result walks. The question remains."

Dremm spat.

One of Vessa's old witnesses smiled, which made me want to throw a stone at his teeth.

Harvek said, "Ask question plain."

Vessa lifted a strip of white bark. "Second pit."

The camp roared.

This time Harvek did not let it rise long. His hand cut down.

"No."

"Not death," Vessa said. "Containment. Witness. Naming. The boy enters a bounded hollow before all tribes. No wound. No hunger. No Khar song. No Fen whisper. No Salt cord. He sits until dawn. If nothing speaks through him, Antler lifts taboo."

"And if something speaks?" Solek asked.

Vessa's eyes stayed on me. "Then all tribes hear what Khar Vane has brought among us."

There it was. Not a pit to kill me. A pit to make me perform monster or prove absence. Both were cages. If I entered and the Red stayed quiet, Vessa would say her containment worked. If it rose, she would say I needed containment forever.

"No," Harvek said again.

But too quickly.

I heard it. So did everyone else.

A chief refusing a trap can still look afraid of the question.

Asha spoke. "If taboo remains, roads close. If roads close, blood follows."

Maren said, "Salt Veyr will not bleed coin because Antler and Khar cannot agree whose omen this is."

"I am not omen," I said.

All eyes turned.

I was so tired of eyes.

Vessa's face softened. That was the cruelest thing she had done yet. "Then prove person."

The Red stirred.

It did not like being invited politely.

I looked at the ground between us. Second pit. Not White Pit. A bounded hollow. Witness until dawn. No wound. No hunger. No song.

Every rule was shaped to make the monster's absence look like Vessa's victory.

So change the rules.

"I will sit," I said.

Dremm made a wounded sound.

Harvek's head turned slowly.

"But not in Antler hollow."

Vessa's mouth tightened.

I pointed to the law racks. "Here."

Solek went very still.

"Dig before Khar law. Shallow enough all see. Deep enough I cannot stand without choosing. Salt marks edge. Fen watches blood. Antler asks. Khar answers. No one owns pit."

The words came rough, ugly, half mine and half borrowed from every person who had trapped me with law. Tovek translated where needed. Yara repeated in Khar tongue, and this time she did not improve it. She let the roughness stay.

Vessa studied me.

For the first time, I saw annoyance under the priest face.

Good.

"A child's compromise," she said.

Maren clicked her tongue. "A cheap one. I like it."

Asha's shells whispered. "Fen-Kin will witness."

Harvek looked at Solek.

Solek said, "Law can hold it."

That settled more than Harvek's yes would have.

They dug at sunset.

Not deep. That mattered. The White Pit had swallowed. This hollow accused. Khar Vane youths cut black soil inside the ring near the law racks. Salt Veyr laid cords at the four edges. Fen-Kin placed bowls of marsh water at north and south. Hollow Antler hung one small antler frame above the eastern lip. Solek put ash at the west.

Four tribes. One hole.

I stepped down when the last light went.

My rib had healed enough to ache instead of stab. My mouth still wore Dremm's bruise. My hands shook, so I put them on my knees where everyone could see.

Vessa stood at the east.

"What are you?" she asked.

The question entered me like cold water.

Rook, I thought.

The Red rose behind the name.

Vessa asked again in another tongue. Then another. Old words. Antler words. Maybe words older than Antler. Each one pressed somewhere different in the blood.

I saw snow that had never fallen on this island. I saw men with blue lips biting their palms over a black river. I saw a god with no face drinking from a bowl made of ribs. I saw my own hands too small, holding a woman's braid. Memory or lie. Blood does not label its gifts.

"What are you?"

Hungry, the Red answered.

My mouth stayed closed.

The night stretched. People shifted. Children slept against old knees. Dremm watched with hatred or grief or both. Cairn arrived sometime after moonrise and stood behind Asha.

Vessa kept asking.

Near dawn, my body began to fail from stillness. Pain is easier when moving. Stillness gives it time to speak. The Red pressed against my teeth.

Let me answer.

No.

They ask.

No.

They fear.

Yes.

Use it.

No.

The eastern sky paled.

Vessa leaned close over the antler frame. Her voice dropped so only those nearest heard.

"If there is a boy, speak."

Cruel, clever woman.

Not monster. Boy. She found the door Cairn had named.

My mouth opened.

The Red waited.

"I am Rook," I said.

Only that.

No thunder. No red light. No god howl. Just my name scraped raw by a night of refusing.

Vessa closed her eyes.

She had not won.

Neither had I.

Solek stepped forward and poured ash into the hollow. Maren cut the Salt cords. Asha took up the marsh bowls. Harvek reached down.

I did not take his hand.

I climbed out myself.

The camp watched.

Dawn touched the antler frame, the law racks, the ash on my knees.

Yara whispered, "That will travel too."

This time I said nothing.

Some things are already gone before you can stop them.
